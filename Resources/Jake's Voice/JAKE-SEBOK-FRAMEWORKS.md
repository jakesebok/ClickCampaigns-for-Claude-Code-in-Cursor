# Jake Sebok: Original Frameworks

> **Purpose:** A catalog of frameworks Jake uses in his teaching. Four are claimed as truly original to Jake: **Rejection as a Filter**, **Selling Fire**, **the war cry phrase**, and **the Tension Compass**. The rest are integrated, adapted, or drawn from other traditions.

---

## Truly Jake's: Original Frameworks (4)

### 1. Rejection as Filter
**Jake's original framing**

- **Core idea:** Rejection isn't failure—it's feedback. It shows who wants you for who you really are vs. who has only ever been in it for who you pretended to be.
- **Application:** "You don't need to fit in everywhere. You just need to belong to yourself."

---

### 2. Selling Fire
**Jake's concept and framework** (from book/offer development)

- **Core problem:** When you complete the hero's journey, you come back with "fire"—wisdom, transformation—but fire is hard to hold. Hard to transfer. The Promethean spirit doesn't easily convert into something monetizable.
- **The pain:** Going back to a day job creates "silent resentment"—the job is stealing time from living your purpose.
- **Selling Fire:** Helping mission-driven creators name what they want, transform desire into a solution, use resistance as a compass, define who they serve, create a compelling vision, overcome limiting beliefs, build offers that convert.
- **Principle:** "When you create a business that requires you to wake up every day and do something that compromises who you really are, all you've built is a prison."

---

### 3. The War Cry Phrase
**Jake's original formulation**

- **Phrase:** "The pain you feel is the war cry of your most authentic self, rebelling against a life that won't allow it to be free."
- **Core idea:** Discomfort isn't a flaw or defect—it's your authentic self signaling that something is wrong, that the life you're living won't allow it to be free. And if you listen, it points the way back home.

---

### 4. The Tension Compass
**Jake's original concept and exercise**

- **Core idea:** The tension in your life isn't random—it's actually pointing to your joy. Where you feel friction, where you're shrinking, where something feels off—trace it and it reveals what you're being called toward.
- **Exercise:** Map tension → decode it → reveal the buried bliss underneath.
- **Key line:** "We're not just naming what feels off. We're revealing the buried bliss that's been hiding underneath."
- **Insight:** "Where the tension in your life is actually pointing to your joy."

## Frameworks Jake Uses (Integrated/Adapted)

These are part of Jake's teaching but are drawn from or adapted from other traditions—not claimed as original.

### Pain as Compass
(Drawn from Tony Robbins, Erickson, others—Jake's war cry phrase makes it distinct; see Truly Jake's above)

- **Core idea:** Discomfort, restlessness, resentment, and tension are not flaws—they're compasses pointing toward what you actually want.
- **Application:** When you feel off, ask: "Where is this trying to take me?" not "What's wrong with me?"

---

### The Voice of Reason

- **Definition:** "The inner PR agent for your persona." The well-behaved, rule-abiding part that explains why your deepest desires are impractical, irresponsible, selfish, unrealistic, or "too much."
- **Key insight:** "What it protects you from is also what it keeps you from." It's the psychological brake pedal.
- **Practice:** Name it, notice its signature (where it shows up in your body), give it a name (Dad, Doubt, the CFO). Then: "You're cool this time. But thanks." And keep going.
- **Contrast:** Not your intuition. Not your truth. "It always sounds reasonable. It uses logic. It makes sense. It feels like maturity. But it's actually fear in disguise."

---

### Yes State

- **Definition:** The embodied feeling when you're fully, unapologetically alive. "Warm, electric, grounded, light—whatever it was. This is your yes state."
- **Key insight:** "It may not be logical, it may not be safe, but it is true."
- **Use:** Contrast with the Voice of Reason. Your yes state is what you're moving toward; the Voice of Reason is what pulls you back.

---

### The System (Three Pressures)

- **1. The Neighbors** — "What will the neighbors think?" Keeping up with the Joneses. Social comparison.
- **2. Institutions** — Cultural concepts of what is right and good. "If I don't participate, I'm a leach on society."
- **3. God / Natural Law** — When morality moves from social order into divine/natural law: fear of being wrong, bad, unloved by God.
- **Insight:** "The mask formed as a strategy to survive these systemic pressures—to secure love, safety, or connection."

---

### The Mask Exercise

- **Three questions:**
  1. What masks do you wear most often?
  2. What do you think the mask is gaining you?
  3. What are you afraid will happen if you show the truth of who you are underneath?
- **Purpose:** Expose masks for what they are; unveil truest self.
- **Follow-up:** Mask Integration with Compassion—integrate rather than attack; meet with honesty and compassion.

---

### Cartesian Questions + Ecology
(Cartesian questions are a classic coaching tool; applied through life ecology lens)

- **Structure:** Four prompts that explore cost of change AND cost of staying the same.
- **Jake's twist:** Apply not just from your own lens, but through **life ecology**—relationships, career, parenting, friendships.
- **Question:** "What happens to all of it if nothing changes?"
- **Purpose:** "When you can see the full weight of what's at stake, clarity comes. Momentum comes. Change begins."

---

### CVF (Compelling Vision of the Future)
**Jake's terminology**

- **Definition:** A vivid, sensory, emotionally resonant image of what aligned life looks like and feels like—not a list of goals, not a 10-year plan, not a vision board with yachts.
- **Why it matters:** "Your subconscious doesn't move away from pain. It moves toward pictures." Without a compelling picture, your inner GPS is aimless.
- **Key principle:** "The subconscious cannot process a negative." Give it a vision worth chasing.

---

### Identity Thermostat / Belief Hacking
**Jake's metaphor and practice**

- **Metaphor:** "Your brain is like a thermostat. It regulates your identity. If you act too far outside that identity, it kicks in and brings you back to baseline."
- **Insight:** "Your brain will never let you become someone it doesn't believe you already are."
- **Practice:** Identify beliefs that shape the thermostat ("I could never make money doing that," "It's selfish to want more"). These become "glass walls" between you and the life you want. Belief Hacking = dissolving those walls.
- **Note:** "Most of these beliefs don't sound dramatic. They sound harmless. But every one becomes a glass wall."

---

### Boundaries Definition
**Jake's formulation**

- **Definition:** "The distance at which I can love both you and me simultaneously."
- **Context:** Used when discussing healthy boundaries as an outcome of the journey.

---

### The Fishing Net
**Jake's metaphor**

- **Image:** Casting a net with small holes catches all the tiny fish—little validations, surface-level friendships, performative relationships—but you're still starving inside.
- **Insight:** "What I really wanted was the big catch—the people who actually see me, my people, the life that actually aligns."
- **Application:** "To catch that, you've gotta let the small stuff fall through. Be willing to be misunderstood, judged, left out by people you never actually belonged to in the first place."

---

### Aligned Life Roadmap
**Jake's strategy framework**

- **Definition:** A strategy that translates vision into real-world steps—not generic productivity hacks, not a 12-week hustle plan. Honors your values, energy, season of life.
- **Principle:** "Clarity doesn't come from thinking, it comes from moving."
- **Metaphor:** "You don't need to know how to cross the entire ocean. You just need to know where to set your sail today."
- **Output:** Name the first most powerful step you can take this week. Not next month. This week.

---

### Purpose: Look Backward, Inward, Forward
**Jake's three-part structure**

- **Look backward** — What shaped you?
- **Look inward** — What drives you?
- **Look forward** — What kind of impact only you can make?
- **Key:** "I'm not asking you to figure out your purpose. I'm asking you to listen for it. It's already there—woven into your story, your pain, your longings, your values."

---

### The What If Visualization
**Jake's guided visualization structure**

- **Structure:** Timeline (past → present → future). Rise above timeline. See fork in the road. Two paths: stay masked vs. choose truth.
- **Time jumps:** 1 year, 5 years, 10 years—still pretending. What do you feel? What's the cost? What did you model for others?
- **Climax:** Future self in mirror. "What would they say? What truth do they need you to hear?"
- **Return:** "You are not that future self. Not yet. And you don't have to ever be. You've seen where this road leads. Now you get to choose."

## Integrated Frameworks (How Jake Combines Them)

Jake layers and combines existing frameworks in his teaching:

### Journey Home Course Architecture
- **5-Module structure** mapped to Hero's Journey stages
- **Treasure Map** — Visual guide of the full journey
- **"You are here"** — Sticker concept for locating self on the map
- **Module flow:** Mask → Cost → Call/Possibility → Vision → Roadmap

### P-R-E-S-E-N-T Exercise
- From NLP; Jake applies it specifically for vision embodiment and Aligned Life Roadmap
- Purpose: Help brain experience vision as if already real; anchor into nervous system; map steps to get there

### Jung + Campbell Integration
- Jake layers Jung's persona/shadow/ego onto Campbell's Hero's Journey
- "The labyrinth is thoroughly known. We have only to follow the thread of the hero path."
- Uses Campbell's structure as the map; Jung's psychology as the terrain
