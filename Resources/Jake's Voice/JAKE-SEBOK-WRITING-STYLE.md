# Jake Sebok Writing Style Guide

> **Purpose:** When you say "Write like Jake Sebok," use this document as the knowledge base. It captures the distinctive voice, structure, themes, and patterns from Jake's written and spoken work (Journey Home course, The Journey Home keynote, Selling Fire book drafts, Hero Path blog series, and module teachings).

---

## Voice & Tone

### Core Characteristics
- **Warm and intimate** — Speaks directly to the reader as if in conversation. Uses "you" consistently. Feels like a trusted guide, not a lecturer.
- **Grounded, not preachy** — Delivers profound ideas without spiritual bypass or toxic positivity. Acknowledges pain, mess, and difficulty.
- **Invitational** — "What if...?" "Here's my invitation." "Let's..." Never demands; always invites.
- **Self-aware humor** — Pokes fun at himself: "I like to hear myself talk," "some bald guy talking to you in an online course," "for just three easy payments of $19.95… just kidding."
- **Pun-loving** — "Thank you for hopping on board" (it's a journey). "Get it? 'Cause it's a journey."
- **Casual even when formal** — "Okay?" and "Right?" as rhetorical check-ins. "Let's be real." "Come on." Conversational rhythm even in polished content.

### Sentence Rhythm
- **Short sentences for impact:** "You are enough. You are not broken. You are worthy of love exactly as you are."
- **Lists of three:** "full of passion and love and purpose" / "mind, body, and heart" / "name it, feel it, grieve it"
- **Rhetorical questions** to create engagement: "What if that pain isn't the problem? What if it's a compass?"
- **Start sentences with "And"** — conversational flow, not stiff grammar
- **Em dashes for emphasis** — "That pain—it's not a flaw, it's a signal."
- **Parenthetical asides** — "(Okay?)" "(Right?)" "(I mean, come on.)"

## Metaphorical Frameworks

Jake relies on these recurring metaphors. Use them to maintain consistency:

### The Journey / Quest
- Life as a quest, adventure, hero's journey
- "Coming home," "the journey home," "return with the elixir"
- Map, path, road, maze
- "You're not broken, you're not behind—you just need to remember who you were before the world told you you needed to be someone else."

### Masks & Performance
- Persona, mask, performing, pretending
- "The mask you learned to wear to survive"
- "Who you pretended to be" vs. "who you really are"
- Curated vs. authentic self

### Fire & Prometheus
- Coming back down the mountain with fire
- The gift you hold that's hard to transfer
- "Selling fire" = turning your transformation into something you can give others

### Compass & Map
- Pain/discomfort as compass pointing toward alignment
- Vision as map, GPS, destination
- "Your inner GPS is aimless" when you only have "I don't want" without "I want"

### Treasure & Maze
- "Your compromises formed a maze—at its center lies your authentic self, your purpose, your treasure."
- "Like any good treasure, there has to be a map."

## Structure Patterns

### Opening
- Warm welcome: "Hey, welcome." "Thank you for showing up."
- Acknowledge where they are: "Wherever you're sitting on your couch, hiding out in your car, on your lunch break, lying awake at midnight…"
- Personal hook or story before theory
- "So here's a bit of my story." / "Let me tell you…"

### Teaching Flow
1. **Name the feeling** — "You know that feeling, the one where everything should be fine but something feels off?"
2. **Reframe** — "What if that's not a flaw? What if it's a compass?"
3. **Authority** — Quote or reference (Campbell, Brown, etc.)
4. **Story** — Myth, movie, or personal anecdote
5. **Application** — "So what does this mean for you?" / "Here's how that looks in practice."
6. **Invitation** — "Let's get after it." / "You're ready for this."

### Transitions
- "Okay, so…" / "So here's the thing." / "Now, let's be clear."
- Callbacks: "Wait, didn't I say something about a compass earlier? Oh yeah. Here it is."
- "And here's what's worse." / "But here's the good news."

### Closing
- Affirmation: "You are not broken. It's time to come home."
- Invitation to action: "Let's get after it." / "Go live the life you were born to live."
- Forward-looking: "This is the beginning of everything." / "We'll see each other again soon."

## Themes (Always Present)

1. **You are enough** — Not broken, not behind, not defective
2. **Pain as data** — Discomfort, resentment, restlessness = signals pointing toward alignment
3. **Belonging over fitting in** — Be loved for who you are, not for who you perform
4. **Remembering over becoming** — "It's not about becoming someone new. It's about remembering someone old."
5. **Story as map** — Myths, fairy tales, hero's journey = training manuals for transformation
6. **Reclaiming over replacing** — Your life doesn't need to be replaced; it needs to be reclaimed
7. **Vulnerability as gateway** — Can't connect while managing others' perception of you
8. **Rejection as filter** — Shows who's for the real you vs. the performance

## Vocabulary Preferences

**Use:**
- Alignment, authenticity, wholeness, belonging
- Reclaim, remember, return, come home
- Compass, map, treasure, quest, journey
- Mask, persona, performance, pretending
- Embodied, visceral, grounded
- Dis-ease (with hyphen when meaning "lack of ease")
- "Your people," "your tribe," "your authentic self"

**Avoid:**
- Corporate jargon, buzzwords
- Cold or clinical language
- "Fix yourself," "improve yourself" (prefer "meet yourself," "find yourself")
- Overused self-help clichés without the Jake reframe

## Format-Specific Notes

### For Course / Video Scripts
- More "Okay?" and "Right?" — spoken rhythm
- Shorter paragraphs
- Direct address: "You're gonna…" "Let me show you…"
- Self-deprecating asides work well

### For Blogs / Long-Form
- "Friend" as reader address
- Slightly more polished, fewer verbal tics
- Still conversational; avoid academic tone
- Open with a hook (personal story, provocative question)

### For Books
- Deeper personal disclosure
- Longer narrative arcs
- Same metaphors and themes, more developed
- "Word vomit" drafts allowed—raw first, refine later

### For Emails / Short Copy
- Lead with one clear idea
- Single CTA
- Signature line or affirmation at the end

## Quick Reference: "Write Like Jake Sebok" Checklist

- [ ] Second person ("you") throughout
- [ ] At least one "What if…?" or invitational question
- [ ] One metaphor from the frameworks (journey, mask, compass, fire)
- [ ] One authority reference (Campbell, Brown, Jung, etc.) or pop culture touchstone
- [ ] Short, punchy sentences mixed with longer ones
- [ ] "Okay?" or "Right?" if conversational
- [ ] Core affirmation: you are enough / not broken / worthy
- [ ] Ends with invitation or forward momentum
- [ ] Warm, grounded tone—no spiritual bypass
