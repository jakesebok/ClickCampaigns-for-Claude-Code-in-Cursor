## Overview

The Clarity Workshop content comes from **Virginia Muskees** (the Referral Diva) and the **Business by Referral** / **Goals That Matter** frameworks. It addresses the core challenge for six-figure solopreneurs: **lack of clarity** — knowing what you do but struggling to articulate it in a way that attracts clients and referral partners.

## 1. Start With Why (Simon Sinek Framework) — 014 Memberships

**Source:** 014 - Memberships.mp4 — Virginia's version of Simon Sinek's TED talk, applied to referral marketing.

### The Golden Circle

| Layer | What It Is | Problem |
|-------|------------|---------|
| **What** | Product, service, job. Easy to identify. | Every company has this. Uninspiring. |
| **How** | Differentiating value prop, proprietary process, unique selling process | Often confused with "why buy from you." The how only tells the consumer their **level of compliance** (DIY, do it with consultant, done for you). |
| **Why** | Purpose, cause, belief. Why does your org exist? Why do you get out of bed? **Why should we care?** | **The secret to marketing, success, and magnetic client attraction.** Very few dig deep enough. |

### Your Why Is NOT

- To make money (that's the purpose of business)
- To help people (too generic)

### Apple Example

- **Ordinary message:** "We make computers. Beautifully designed, user-friendly, productive." → Buy on price.
- **Apple's message:** "Everything we do is about innovation. We challenge the status quo. We believe in thinking differently. The way we do that is [how] — and it happens to be a computer."
- Apple **never competes on price**. Their customers think like they think, believe what they believe. "When somebody buys a PC, does Apple cry? Thank goodness—they're not my customer."
- **First to claim it, you own it.** Nordstrom = customer service. Walmart = low prices. Apple = innovation.

### Unique Buying Proposition

Not only your why—but the **consumer's why** for the purchase. (Brennan Scanlon / Referral Institute concept.) Apple: People didn't want storage—they wanted portable music. "Put a thousand songs in your pocket."

### Key Insight for Referral Marketing

*"The whole point of referral marketing—it starts with relationship. It starts with knowing who you are, knowing what you think, valuing what you value, and standing for that. Apple is unapologetic. You don't like innovation? Okay. I have plenty of people who don't want to be my client. I have plenty who do."*

### Who's In Your Room (Dr. Ivan Meisner)

If everyone you let in never leaves—who would you remove? You need **criterion**. The only way to know who to let in is to **know who you are and what you value**.

## 2. Starting Point (Knowing Where You Are)

**Core idea:** You need to know where you start before you can chart a course to where you're going.

### Key Questions to Answer

| Category | Questions |
|----------|-----------|
| **Origin** | Why did you start this business? What motivated you? (Corporate exit, downsizing, feeling called?) |
| **Ideal Customer** | Who is your #1 customer? Pick one from your history who represents your ideal. |
| **Demographics** | Gender, age, education, income, zip code, car, children, etc. |
| **Psychographics** | What makes them a joy to work with? Humor, patience, collaboration? |
| **Problems Solved** | What problems do clients come to you with? (Not products—solutions. Like cell phone: communication, entertainment, safety.) |
| **Products/Services** | What do you use to solve those problems? |
| **Profit + Joy** | Which aspects bring the most profit AND joy? (No sense making money if miserable.) |
| **Unique Edge** | What do customers say they can't get anywhere else? (Hint: not "great customer service.") |
| **Vision** | Where do you want to be in 3–5 years? What do you want to do, have, become? |
| **12-Month Goals** | Specific measurable goals for the next 12 months. How much increase over prior 12 months? |

**Quote:** *"If you knew what you wanted to say, you'd be able to say it."* — Mrs. Martinez

## 3. Goals That Matter (GTM) — 5-Day Framework

### The Three Levels of Goals

| Level | Name | Description | Energy |
|-------|------|-------------|--------|
| **1** | **Needy Goals** | Survival goals. Bills, baseline revenue. Set from lack. | Fear, anxiety, exhaustion. **Client repellent.** |
| **2** | **Greedy Goals** | Lifestyle goals. Affluence, abundance, finer things. | Often blocked by "you don't deserve it" programming. |
| **3** | **MAD Goals** | Make A Difference goals. Impact, contribution, mission. | Curiosity, trust, generosity. **Client magnet.** |

### GTM Day 1: Know Your Numbers

- **Gross sales** (last 12 months): new + repeat + recurring revenue
- **Business expenses** (last 12 months)
- **Net profit** = gross sales − expenses (personal income for solopreneurs)

**Problem:** Hoping leftover money covers bills = stressful, desperate energy = client repellent. *"No one invests in the energy of desperate."*

**Homework:** Get 12-month income statement. Calculate the three numbers. Rate satisfaction (1–10) if that number stayed the same next year.

---

### GTM Day 2: Baseline Sales Goal (Needy → Neutral)

- **Personal expenses** (last 12 months): bank statements, total outflow
- **Baseline sales goal** = personal expenses + business expenses

**Example:** $60K personal + $42K business = **$102K baseline**

**Key insight:** Reframe needy goals as neutral baseline. Don't attach fear to them. Fear repels; neutrality allows forward motion.

---

### GTM Day 3: Greedy Goals (Lifestyle)

**Mindset gap:** We set lifestyle visions but pursue survival sales goals. We're programmed to conflate "grateful for what you have" with "don't want more."

**Fix:**
1. Brainstorm everything you'd like to do/have/be in 5 years (5 min, no censoring)
2. When the voice says "you don't deserve that" → answer: *"I am grateful for what I have AND I still want this."*
3. Highlight the 3 things you're most excited about
4. Choose the ONE you'll give yourself permission to achieve in 12 months
5. Estimate cost + add 50% (taxes, expenses, investment)
6. Add that number to baseline = new sales goal

**Example:** Cruise $10K + 50% = $15K greedy goal. $102K baseline + $15K = **$117K**

---

### GTM Day 4: MAD Goals (Make A Difference)

- Add **at least 10%** to your greedy goal for impact
- **MAD revenue goal** = survival + lifestyle + make a difference

**Choose an impact project that:**
- Grabs you by the throat (poverty, addiction, homelessness, animal welfare, environment, etc.)
- Aligns with your core values and work
- You'll fight for even when exhausted

**Example:** *"As a supporter of [org], I commit to contributing my time, talent, and at least 10% of my revenue goal of $X to [mission]."*

**Alignment matters:** Virginia's 10by3 (artisans → entrepreneurs) aligns with her entrepreneurship focus. A whale retreat leader might choose ocean cleanup. Someone in recovery might support addiction prevention.

---

### GTM Day 5: The One Number — Sales Conversations Needed

**Core formula:** How many qualified sales conversations do you need to hit your MAD goal?

**Four calculations:**

1. **Average client value** = gross revenue ÷ number of active clients (last 12 months)
   - Example: $50K ÷ 20 clients = $2,500/client

2. **Clients needed** = MAD goal ÷ client value
   - Example: $117K ÷ $2,500 = 47 clients

3. **Close ratio** = new clients onboarded ÷ total sales conversations
   - Example: 10 clients ÷ 50 conversations = 0.2 (20%)

4. **Conversations needed** = clients needed ÷ close ratio
   - Example: 47 ÷ 0.2 = **235 conversations**

**This number** = your focus for the next 12 months. Put it on a Post-it. Track it.

**Why MAD goals work:** When you focus on making a difference, prospects feel curiosity and trust—not scarcity. Your energy shifts from need/greed to generosity. *"Desperation is not magnetic."*

**Warning:** You must actually deliver on your MAD commitment. Congruence matters. If you don't, it reads as manipulation, not influence.

**MAD goal sizing (Memberships workshop):** Make your MAD goal **equal or exceed** your greedy goal. That way the "guilt guy" in your head feels balanced—you got something for yourself AND made a big difference.

## 4. Memberships Workshop — Live Format Additions

The **Memberships** video is a ~47-minute live workshop that reinforces the GTM framework with additional nuance:

### Referrals vs. Sales Conversations

- **Referral** = someone brings the person to you and hands them to you (not cold outreach)
- Closing ratio depends on **referral quality**. A name + number from a stranger ≠ a warm intro from someone who understands your ideal client
- If you're not closing 1 in 2 or 1 in 3 referrals, your network needs training—they don't understand what you need

### Vision Board Reframe

- Put a **vision board of how you're changing the world** on your wall—not boats, kitchens, new shoes
- *"What will the world look like one year from now because you did this?"*

### Key Quotes

- **Zig Ziglar:** *"When you help enough other people get what they need, you will have everything that you want."*
- **Be insufficient:** *"If you feel inadequate to the task, good—you can attract people who will help you. If you're sufficient, you don't need anyone."*
- **Money as energy:** *"Money is energy and energy circulates. The minute we hoard, the quality of our life decreases. Allow flow to us and through us."*

### Making the Number Accessible

- 800 referrals ÷ 40 weeks = 20 referrals/week
- *"What if I knew 20 people who would each give me a referral a week?"*
- Crowdfunding (Indiegogo, etc.) as a way to support world-changing projects with small amounts

## 5. MIQs — Most Important Questions (Vishan Lachiani)

From *The Secret Spiritual Art of Succeeding at Work*. Used for ongoing clarity and life design.

### Means Goals vs. End Goals

- **Means goals:** Achievement-oriented (sell 5 programs, write a book, run a marathon)
- **End goals:** Why you're here. Transformation. What you want accomplished by the time you leave this earth. (Steven Covey: "Write your eulogy.")

**Idea:** Design your life. Don't sacrifice dreams to make money—craft a way of living that includes both.

### The Three Most Important Questions

| # | Question | Purpose |
|---|----------|---------|
| 1 | **What do you really want to experience in your life?** | Dreams list. Experiences. (Cirque du Soleil, 7-continent cruise, write a screenplay.) |
| 2 | **How do you want to grow or develop yourself?** | What do you want to learn? Skills, knowledge, development. |
| 3 | **How do you want to contribute to the world?** | Impact. Mission. Leaving joy in your wake. |

**Practice:** Freeform journal ~4 minutes per question. Weekly: *"What else do I want to experience? How else do I want to grow? How else can I contribute?"*

**Note:** When the voice says "you can't have that"—put it out there anyway. Often you realize you could learn, or you know people who know people. The act of naming it opens possibilities.

## 6. Key Frameworks for Campaign Work

### For Brand Clarity / Brand Knowledge Base

- Why they started the business
- Ideal customer (demographic + psychographic)
- Problems solved (not features—solutions)
- Products/services that solve those problems
- Unique edge (what clients say they can't get elsewhere)
- 3–5 year vision
- 12-month measurable goals

### For Messaging / Positioning

- **Start with Why** — Lead with purpose, cause, belief. Not what you do or how you do it.
- Lead with MAD (make a difference) energy, not needy or greedy
- "I'm committed to [impact] by helping [audience] build [outcome]"
- Include the contribution/mission in your story—it creates curiosity and trust
- Be unapologetic about your why. You're not for everybody.

### For Goal-Setting Exercises

- Baseline (survival) → Greedy (lifestyle) → MAD (impact)
- The one number: sales conversations needed
- MIQs for ongoing clarity and life design
