# Home Page & About Page — Full Team Copy Review

**Reviewers:** Ryan (Direct Response Copywriter), Jordan (Website Strategist), Alexis (Funnel Architect)  
**Date:** March 2025

---

## Executive Summary

The copy is strong—authentic, differentiated, and aligned with Jake's values. The philosophy (peaks vs. valleys, sustainable high performance, alignment over output) is clear and consistent. The team identified opportunities to sharpen headlines, reduce redundancy, improve CTA hierarchy, and add specificity in a few areas.

---

# Ryan — Direct Response Copywriter

## Home Page

### Hero
**Headline:** "Stop building someone else's success."  
- **Strong.** Provocative, memorable, creates a clear "before" state. The "someone else's" emphasis works—it implies you're building the wrong thing, not that you're not building.
- **Subhead:** The discipline/alignment reframe is excellent. The "peak performance" distinction is now woven in well. One note: "fueled by what you actually want at the core of who you are" is slightly abstract. Consider: "fueled by what you actually want—not what you think you should want." More concrete.

**Quote:** "Your business shouldn't be a beautiful prison. It should be the best expression of who you actually are."  
- **Strong.** Distinctive, quotable. "Beautiful prison" is a great image.

**CTAs:** "Take the VAPI™" (primary) — "See How It Works" (secondary)  
- **Good hierarchy.** Clear next step. Consider testing "Start the VAPI™" for slightly softer commitment language.

### The Cost of Success
**Headline:** "From the outside, it looks like success. But you know the cost."  
- **Strong.** Creates empathy and curiosity.

**Intro:** "For impact-driven founders who are done white-knuckling their way to the next level—sound familiar?"  
- **Good.** Narrows the audience. The em dash before "sound familiar?" works—it's a pause. Minor: "sound familiar?" could be "does this sound familiar?" for slightly more conversational flow.

**Card copy:** The Grind, The Guilt, The Loop—all punchy. "The business runs on you, and you're running on fumes" is excellent. "Plan. Start. Stall. Repeat." is tight. No changes needed.

### The Work
**Headline:** "I don't optimize for output. I optimize for alignment."  
- **Strong.** Clear differentiator.

**Intro:** "It doesn't have to stay this way. Awareness. Internal Alignment. Embodied Execution—the path from the cage to the life you actually want."  
- **Good.** "The cage" echoes the CTA. "Awareness. Internal Alignment. Embodied Execution" reads like a list—consider adding "and" before "Embodied Execution" for flow: "Awareness, Internal Alignment, and Embodied Execution."

**Card copy:** "Surface what's actually going on—not what you think you should want" — clear. "NLP-based parts work that turns self-sabotage into momentum" — good. "Sustainable growth that doesn't require you to abandon what you actually want" — strong. All solid.

### By the End of Our Work Together
**Headline:** "You will have"  
- **Functional but could be stronger.** "You will have" is a bit passive. Alternatives: "By the end of our work together, you will:" or "Here's what you'll have when we're done:" — more direct.

**Outcome #2:** "Knowledge of your greatest constraints and the single most valuable levers" — slightly long. Consider: "Clarity on your greatest constraints and the single most valuable levers to pull."

**Outcome #5:** "Healthier, sharper, and more energized than you have in years" — strong. "Because you're finally doing the work only YOU can do" — excellent payoff.

### Testimonials
**Headline:** "Real transformation. Real results."  
- **Good.** Used on both homepage and about page. Could differentiate: "What happens when alignment replaces the grind." — but "Real transformation. Real results." is fine if you want consistency.

### CTA Section
**Headline:** "Ready to stop building the cage?"  
- **Strong.** Ties back to hero and The Work. "The cage" is now a recurring motif—good.

**Body:** "72 questions. ~12 minutes. An honest snapshot." — clear. "Free. No payment required." — good risk reversal.

## About Page

### Hero
**Headline:** "Hey, I'm Jake Sebok."  
- **Warm, conversational.** "Hey" works for an About page. Some audiences prefer "Hi" or "I'm Jake"—test if needed.

**Subhead:** "Master Certified Professional Coach, founder, full-time dad to three amazing kids."  
- **Good.** Credentials + humanity. "Amazing" is a bit generic—could be "three kids" or "three little ones" for variety, but it's fine.

### Story
**Body copy:** The arc (achievement → burnout → wake-up → rebuild) is clear and relatable. "I was giving my family the scraps" is strong. "And the worst part? I knew something was wrong. I just couldn't stop." — excellent.

**Suggestion:** "I'm here to be relentlessly honest and fully committed to helping you build what only you can—as you bring your gifts to the world." — "as you bring your gifts to the world" feels slightly tacked on. Consider: "I'm here to be relentlessly honest and fully committed to helping you build what only you can build."

**Closing:** "Believe me, friends—your best days are ahead. Let's do this together."  
- **Warm, confident.** "Believe me, friends" is a bit folksy—could be "Trust me" or "I believe your best days are ahead" for a slightly more formal tone. But it's authentic—keep if it matches Jake's voice.

### Credentials
**Headline:** "I'm not an observer. I'm a survivor and thriver on this path."  
- **Strong.** Differentiates from academic coaches.

**Cards:** "Trained at the highest level" / "Proven in the arena" — good. "20x in nine months" is specific. "I've always prioritized people over metrics—and the results followed" — strong.

### How I Work
**Headline:** "I coach the whole human being—not just the business."  
- **Strong.** Clear differentiator.

**Body:** "Where competitors optimize for output, I optimize for alignment" — echoes homepage. "This isn't peak performance. It's sustainable performance—because peaks imply valleys" — consistent with homepage. Good.

### Foundations
**Headline:** "Built on thinkers who understood the human journey."  
- **Good.** "Howard Thurman. Joseph Campbell. Carl Jung. Brené Brown." — strong authority. "I help you let go of old identities and return to the fire that was always yours" — excellent.

### Proof
**Headline:** "Transformation isn't theoretical. Here's what clients say when the work is done."  
- **Strong.** Clear, specific.

# Jordan — Website Strategist

## Conversion & Flow

### Home Page
**Overall flow:** Pain → Solution → Outcomes → Proof → CTA. Logical. Good.

**CTA count:** Two primary CTAs (Take the VAPI™) — hero and final section. One secondary (See How It Works / See All Offerings). Appropriate. Not overwhelming.

**CTA placement:** The final CTA section is strong—certifications, clear value prop, urgency-free. "Free. No payment required" reduces friction. Good.

**Suggestion:** Add a soft CTA after the Outcomes section? Something like "Ready to see where you stand?" with a link to the VAPI. The Outcomes section creates desire—a CTA there could capitalize on that moment. Not a full-width button—maybe a subtle text link or a small "Take the VAPI™" pill. Test.

**Trusted By strip:** "Trusted by Doctors, Coaches, Healers..." — good social proof. No logos—just categories. Could add "and more" if you want to broaden without listing. Fine as is.

### About Page
**Flow:** Story → Credentials → How I Work → Foundations → Proof → CTA. Good.

**CTA:** "Work With Me" at the end. Appropriate. Single CTA is fine for an About page. Consider: could the About page also link to "Take the VAPI™" for visitors who want to self-assess before committing? Optional. "Work With Me" is the right primary CTA for someone who's already read the About page.

**Cross-linking:** About page links to Work With Me. Could it also link to Case Studies? "See how Marshall went from dreading work to excited every day" — adds proof and creates a path to conversion. Worth adding.

### Cross-Page Consistency
- "Real transformation. Real results." appears on both Home and About (testimonials). Fine.
- "Peak performance vs. sustainable performance" appears on both. Good consistency.
- "Alignment over output" appears on both. Good.

# Alexis — Funnel Architect

## Funnel & Messaging

### Home Page
**Entry point:** Home is the top of funnel. Traffic may come from ads, organic, referrals. The hero assumes some awareness—"you're building someone else's success" implies they know they're building something. Good for warm traffic. For cold traffic, the "Cost of Success" section does the heavy lifting.

**Messaging ladder:** Pain (Cost of Success) → Solution (The Work) → Outcomes (By the end) → Proof (Testimonials) → CTA. Correct structure.

**Objection handling:** "Plan. Start. Stall. Repeat. You've tried the discipline fixes. They didn't stick." — addresses the "I've tried coaching" objection. Good. Could add explicit objection handling for "I'm skeptical of coaching" — Marshall's story on the Case Studies page does this. Consider a testimonial on the homepage from someone who was skeptical. Thaddeus or Marshall could work.

**Offer clarity:** The VAPI is the lead magnet. Free. Low commitment. Good. "See How It Works" is the secondary path for people who want to understand before taking the assessment. Clear.

### About Page
**Role in funnel:** About is typically for people who are already interested—they want to know who Jake is before they trust him. The story (burnout → rebuild) builds credibility and relatability. Good.

**Credibility stacking:** Credentials are strong. "How I Work" differentiates. "Foundations" adds intellectual lineage. Good layering.

**Proof:** Two testimonials. Could add "Read more case studies" or "See Marshall's full story" to link to Case Studies. Adds depth.

### Cross-Funnel Consistency
**Home → About:** Both reinforce the same message (alignment, sustainable performance, not peak performance). Good.

**Home → Work With Me:** Home says "See How It Works" — Work With Me is the logical next step. Good.

**Home → Assessment:** Primary CTA. Clear.

**About → Work With Me:** CTA at end. Good.

**Missing link:** Case Studies is not prominently linked from Home or About. It's in the nav. Consider: add a "See Marshall's transformation" or "Read case studies" link in the Proof section on Home, or in the About Proof section. Case Studies is a powerful conversion asset—it should be easier to find from the main flow.
