# Video 1.0 — The Theory of Aligned Power

**Module 1: Awareness | 12–18 minutes**

---

## VIDEO SCRIPT

Hey. Before we go any further—before the assessments, before the worksheets, before we dig into your scores—I want to give you the underlying theory of what we're actually doing here. Because if you don't get this, the rest of it will feel like another self-improvement program. And that's not what this is.

---

### The Question That Changes Everything

Here's the most important question a business owner can ask themselves:

**In what ways would achieving your current definition of success threaten your happiness, your health, your relationships, or your sense of freedom?**

Another way to ask it: *How are the ways you're currently working to attain your goals keeping you from ever truly achieving them?*

Why does that matter? Because you're not the one making the decisions in your life. Not in the way you think.

---

### 95% Subconscious — The Little Scientist

Roughly 95% of human behavior is driven by the subconscious. That's Latin for "below awareness"—in other words, not known to us.

I like to imagine the subconscious as a little scientist with Einstein hair, running around the control center of my mind like in *Inside Out*. His whole job is to keep me safe. He's constantly running little experiments to see if something is dangerous or not.

Also… he's a toddler.

Yeah. He's not too concerned with abstract notions of prestige and career aspirations. His main goals are fun, feel-goods, freedom, stuff like that. And if he's got his hands on the master control switch of what I actually do, anytime he sees potential danger, he's going to sabotage the heck out of it.

When your conscious mind says "I want to build a business that matters" and your subconscious is still running the script that says "you're not safe unless you perform"—guess who wins? The subconscious. Every time.

---

### The Hero Complex — The Limiting Belief That Keeps You Stuck

Here's one of the most common limiting beliefs that keeps people from growing or achieving their goals.

Someone with a hero complex—hyper-self-reliant, the one who has to do it all—thinks something like this: *If making 100k per year feels like this, making 300k per year must feel 3x harder.*

So they don't delegate. They can't. Because in their mind, scaling means scaling *themselves*. More success means more of the same grind. More hours. More weight on their shoulders. The future isn't a haven—it's a prison that gets bigger the more they achieve.

**Their future becoming a haven instead of a prison depends on learning how to delegate.** Not because delegation is a nice-to-have. Because it's the only way their subconscious will ever let them actually get there. The little scientist sees "more of the same, but worse" and sabotages. Every time.

There are so many limiting beliefs like this. Beliefs that keep you spinning. That make you work hard and go nowhere. That make you build and then sabotage. This is one of the biggest.

---

### Larry — When Success Would Have Been a Prison

Let me tell you about a client. We'll call him Larry.

Larry is a chiropractor. When I started working with him, I asked the same question every coach asks: "What do you want? What do you really want?"

He said: "I want more people in the door. I want to increase leads and the number of paying clients so I can make the kind of money that I desire."

So I hit him with a follow-up: "What are you doing to make that happen?"

He said: "Well, that's the thing, Jake. I know exactly what to do. But every time I go to do it, I end up on TikTok or feel like I want to take a nap or I just stare at a wall instead of doing the thing that would actually help me achieve my goals."

At that point, I already knew what was going on. This is a classic expression of a conflict of interests.

On the outside, everything looks cogent. Business owner wants more money. Nothing weird about that. Usually when they're not doing what they say they want, it's either a strategy problem—which he already told me it wasn't when he said he knew exactly what to do—or a them-problem. This was definitely a them-problem.

I switched gears and asked him why he became a chiropractor to begin with. He could have done anything with his time and his talents, but he chose this.

He told me about his athletic history. How he'd always played sports and still does. But that almost ended in high school when he sustained a concussion. The process of navigating recovery was nearly impossible. Until he met one person who changed everything. He recovered. He built back better. He got that scholarship. His whole future—the one he'd imagined might never be possible—came back to him.

He wanted to do *that* for others. That was what lit him. What made his heart sing. What set his soul on fire.

Next question: "Larry, who are you serving now?"

"Primarily an aging population. I help them with pain management."

"I see. And if you got more clients like that in the door—would that get you closer or further away from the life and business you dreamed of?"

I don't need to keep going. You see it. If he had actually achieved his stated goal, it would have been a prison. It would have kept him from his fiancée, his hobbies, his future—all while doing something he first silently and then loudly resented. Burned out. Too many hours. The wrong people.

Most of us are engaged in something like this. We're working toward a goal, and some part of us is sabotaging our ability to achieve it. Not because we're broken. Because the little scientist sees danger. And he's right.

---

### The Real Reason You're Depressed, Burned Out, or Anxious

So here's the real reason—and it's not what you think.

It's not that you're doing too much. And it's certainly not that you're not doing enough. You're doing the *wrong* things.

You've been living a script that someone else wrote and convinced yourself that you're the character. Every time you fall short of the script, you think it's your problem. But it's not.

That job you hate. The relationship that doesn't quite fit. Being the strong one or the smart one or the one who has to do it all—whatever it is for you—it takes an enormous amount of energy to pretend to be that person. To perform that role.

Jim Carrey once said that depression is your body saying: *I'm done. Done playing the role. Done wearing the costume. I don't want to be this character anymore. It's too much for me.*

Sometimes the body just says: I'm not doing this anymore. That's where the depression and the anxiety and the burnout come in.

---

### The Ketchup Metaphor

Most of what you've been taught to ask is: "What can I do to finally be enough?" That's the wrong question.

Imagine you're driving along and your car loses power. You peek at the gauge and your tank is empty. So you fill it up. With ketchup.

Sounds ridiculous, right? And yet—that's exactly what so many of us do every single day. We're running on anxiety, burnout, disconnection. We're wondering what we can do to finally be enough. But more of the same will never work—because what you've been doing was never made for you. Not the *real* you.

---

### Milton Erickson — Rapport With the Subconscious

The hypnotherapist Milton Erickson put it simply: **being out of rapport with the subconscious is so much of the problem.** Not the whole problem. So much of it.

When you're in rapport—when your conscious goals and your subconscious beliefs are aligned—you move. You don't have to white-knuckle it. You don't have to motivate yourself through guilt. You just… go. The whole system is pointing the same direction.

When you're out of rapport—when part of you wants to grow and part of you is terrified of what happens if you do—you spin. You work hard and go nowhere. You build and then sabotage. You get close and then pull back. That's not a character flaw. That's a system in conflict.

Larry and I worked together to dismantle his current offer, restructure it, build a program specifically for his dream client, increase the price, decrease his time investment, and go from scrolling TikTok to living his passion. We aligned his business with his nervous system. Everything flowed from there.

---

### What We're Actually Doing

So what is the Aligned Power Accelerator—and specifically, Strategic Clarity, the Alignment Arc—actually for?

**We're excavating the real person.** Not manufacturing one. Not helping you become someone new. Excavating the one that's been there the whole time, running the show from below the surface.

And then we're aligning your *actions* with who you *are*—instead of who you think you're *supposed* to be. That includes the limiting beliefs. The hero complex. The "if 100k feels like this, 300k must feel 3x harder." We surface those. We work with them. Because your future becoming a haven instead of a prison depends on it.

Your Real Reasons, your Driving Fire, your values, The Future You, The Revenue Bridge, your Vital Action—all of it exists to surface what's already true and build a life and business that actually fits it.

---

### What We're NOT Doing

We're not here to help you find your authentic self in some mythical sense. We're not doing a hero's journey to a hidden treasure. We're not asking you to become someone you've never been.

We're asking you to stop performing the version of you that was designed to survive—and start operating from the one that was there before the world convinced you to wear the mask. We're asking you to question the beliefs that keep your future a prison instead of a haven.

When you do that—when you get back in rapport with the real you—you go from trying to run on ketchup to being driven by the truth. And that changes everything.

Let's get after it.
