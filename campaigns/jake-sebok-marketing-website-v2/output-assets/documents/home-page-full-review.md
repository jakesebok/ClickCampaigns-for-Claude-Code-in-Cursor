# Home Page — Full Team Review & Optimization

**Reviewers:** Ryan (Direct Response Copywriter), Jordan (Website Strategist), Alexis (Funnel Architect)  
**Date:** March 2025

---

## Executive Summary

The home page is strong—clear flow, differentiated messaging, and consistent use of the alignment reframe. The team identified opportunities to add more orange accent moments, strengthen transitions, reduce redundancy, and tighten a few copy spots. Overall structure is sound; optimizations are incremental.

---

# Ryan — Direct Response Copywriter

## What's Working

- **Hero:** "Stop building someone else's success" — punchy. Orange on "someone else's" creates emphasis. Quote ("beautiful prison") is distinctive.
- **Reframe section:** "You have an alignment problem" with orange on "alignment" — clear, memorable.
- **The Work:** "Optimize for alignment" — command structure works. Three pillars are outcome-focused.
- **Have it all:** "Really." in orange — lands. Jake + son image supports the message.
- **Outcomes:** Bullets are specific. Image on right creates visual balance.
- **CTA:** "Ready to stop building the cage?" — ties back to hero. "cage" in orange works.

## Copy Optimizations

1. **Cost of Success headline** — Add orange accent: "But you know the **cost**." Emphasizes the pain.
2. **Reframe transition** — "That's the work. Ready to see how?" feels slightly flat. Stronger: "That's the work—and here's how we get there." or "That's the work. Here's how."
3. **Have it all** — "You can have it all" appears twice (headline + closing). Consider varying the close: "The work we do together is built on that belief." stands alone; could add "No trade-offs." for punch.
4. **Testimonials section** — "What People Say" / "Real transformation. Real results." — headline could be more specific: "What happens when alignment replaces the grind." (Differentiates from generic testimonial sections.)
5. **The Work intro** — "the path from the cage to the life you actually want" — could add orange on "cage" to echo the motif.

# Jordan — Website Strategist

## Conversion & Flow

- **CTA hierarchy:** Primary (Take the VAPI™) appears in hero, Outcomes, and final CTA. Secondary (See How It Works / See All Offerings) is clear. Good.
- **Section flow:** Pain → Reframe → Solution → Philosophy → Outcomes → Proof → CTA. Logical. No dead ends.
- **Case Studies link:** After testimonials — good placement. Creates path for proof-seekers.
- **Trusted By strip:** Doctors, Coaches, Healers, etc. — builds credibility. No logos; categories only. Fine.

## Recommendations

1. **Add orange accent to Cost of Success** — Low effort, high impact. "cost" is the key word.
2. **Ensure The Work section has visible background** — Currently may inherit body bg. If it blends with Cost of Success, consider explicit `bg-ap-bg` or `bg-white` for clear section separation.
3. **Outcomes CTA** — "Ready to see where you stand?" + Take the VAPI™ — good. Consider adding a subtle "or explore how it works" link for those not ready to commit.

# Alexis — Funnel Architect

## Funnel Analysis

- **Entry:** Hero assumes some awareness. Cost of Success does heavy lifting for cold traffic.
- **Reframe:** "Alignment problem" — correct diagnosis before solution. Reduces resistance.
- **Solution:** Three pillars (Awareness, Internal Alignment, Embodied Execution) — clear, outcome-focused.
- **Philosophy:** "Have it all" — addresses "I'll have to sacrifice" objection. Well placed.
- **Proof:** Testimonials + Case Studies link. Marshall story is prominent. Good.
- **Offer:** VAPI (free) → Work With Me. Clear path.

## Recommendations

1. **Bridge from Reframe to The Work** — The transition "That's the work. Ready to see how?" could be stronger. It's the hinge between problem and solution.
2. **Objection handling** — "Have it all" section handles sacrifice objection. Consider adding "skeptical of coaching?" nod—Marshall's story on Case Studies does this; could add a testimonial from a skeptic to carousel if available.
3. **Consistency** — "cage" motif appears in hero quote, Reframe, The Work intro, CTA. Good. Ensure it's used consistently.
