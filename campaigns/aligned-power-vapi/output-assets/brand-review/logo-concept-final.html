The geometry

Each of the three triangles is a true inscribed equilateral — every vertex lands exactly on the circumference of its containing circle. The outer triangle spans the full circle. The middle triangle is inscribed at 65% of the radius. The inner triangle at 35%. The orange center dot sits at the precise geometric centroid of the circle — the exact same point that is equidistant from all three inner triangle vertices. The orange is the core: in the Detonation brand system, the teal holds structure, the ink holds authority, and the orange detonates from the center outward.

The dashed horizontal tier lines at each triangle's base give the mark an architectural, engineered quality — like a technical drawing of something ancient. The circle contains it all: the outer boundary, the whole, the field within which the ascent happens.

Scale behavior

The mark holds extremely well down to 28px. Below that, the three nested triangles begin to merge — at 20px and 16px the orange center dot and inner triangle dominate, which still reads as a distinct mark. The tier dashes are dropped below 40px (they disappear at small scales) but the core geometry remains legible. Recommendation: use the full mark down to 28px; below that, consider using the isolated inner triangle + orange dot as a micro-icon variant.
