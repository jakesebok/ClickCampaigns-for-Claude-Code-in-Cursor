## Lesson 1.1 — From numbers to names: what your arenas and domains actually mean

**Lesson goal:** Turn the VAPI numbers into clear, judgment-free language. The score isn’t a grade; it’s a signal. Each arena and each domain has a specific meaning—naming it is the first step to changing it.

---

### Opening (talking points)

- You’ve got a set of numbers: scores across 12 domains and 3 arenas. Maybe one or two numbers jumped out—high or low. Maybe it felt like a report card. Here’s the shift: **those numbers aren’t labels. They’re names.**
- The VAPI isn’t telling you you’re broken. It’s telling you *where* things are off so you can stop guessing and start addressing what’s actually going on.
- In this lesson we’re going to go from “I have a 4.2 in Execution” to “I’m leaking energy in how I execute—I’m busy but not aligned.” That’s the move: **from numbers to names.**

---

### Part 1 — The three arenas: Personal, Relationships, Business

**Talking points:**

- Your scores are grouped into **three arenas**: **Personal**, **Relationships**, and **Business**. Each one is a different slice of your life and work.
- **Personal** is about how you’re showing up for yourself: your physical health, inner alignment, mental and emotional state, attention and focus. When this arena is low, you’re often running on fumes or ignoring what you need—and it shows everywhere else.
- **Relationships** is about how you’re showing up for others and the world: relationship to self, family, community, impact. When this is off, you might feel isolated, disconnected, or like you’re performing instead of connecting.
- **Business** is about how you’re showing up in your work and systems: vision and strategy, execution, operational health, ecology. When this is misaligned, you’re often working hard but not moving the needle—or moving it in a direction that doesn’t feel right.
- So when you look at your arena scores, you’re not just looking at “a number.” You’re looking at *where* you’re aligned and *where* you’re leaking energy. **Naming the arena** is the first step: “My Business arena is where I’m most stuck” or “My Personal arena is the one that’s costing me the most.”

---

### Part 2 — Your lowest domains: putting words to the gaps

**Talking points:**

- Within each arena you have **domains**—specific areas the VAPI measures. Your **lowest** domain scores are the places where you’re most out of alignment. Not “bad”—just *off*. And when something is off, it costs you: energy, clarity, time, peace of mind.
- So instead of “I scored low in Execution,” we say: **“I’m leaking energy in execution—I’m doing a lot but not the right things, or not in a way that’s sustainable.”** Instead of “My Relationship to Self is a 4,” we say: **“I’m out of alignment with how I treat myself and what I need—I’m last on my own list.”**
- The point isn’t to shame yourself. It’s to **name the gap**. Once you have the name, you can stop spinning in “what’s wrong with me” and start asking “what would it look like to bring this domain back into alignment?”
- Take a look at your own results. Which arena is your lowest? Which one or two domains are your lowest? **Say it out loud or write it down in plain language.** For example: “My lowest arena is Personal. The domains that are most off are Mental/Emotional and Attention & Focus—I’m not giving myself the space to think clearly or protect my focus.”

## Lesson 1.2 — The real gap isn’t “try harder”—it’s alignment

**Lesson goal:** Introduce the idea that execution without alignment is why they’re stuck. Effort and tactics aren’t the fix when the root issue is misalignment. This sets up the rest of the course (diagnosis, cost, vision, path).

---

### Opening (talking points)

- You’ve named your gaps. Now here’s the shift that changes everything: **the real gap isn’t that you’re not trying hard enough. It’s that you’re not aligned.**
- A lot of high-achievers hear “your score is low in X” and think: I need to do more. Work harder. Get more disciplined. But the VAPI isn’t measuring effort. It’s measuring **alignment**—the fit between what you value, how you’re built, and where you’re putting your energy. When that fit is off, more effort often makes things worse: more burnout, more spinning, more “why isn’t this working?”
- So the fix isn’t “try harder.” It’s **get aligned.** Name what’s off, diagnose what’s underneath, and then put your energy in the right places. That’s what this course is for.

---

### Part 1 — Why execution without alignment keeps you stuck

**Talking points:**

- **Execution** is doing the work: showing up, following through, shipping. **Alignment** is doing the *right* work, in the *right* way, for the *right* reasons. You can execute like crazy and still be stuck—if you’re executing on the wrong thing, or in a way that doesn’t match who you are or what you want.
- Think about it: have you ever pushed harder and harder and still felt like you were going nowhere? Or hit a goal and felt empty? That’s execution without alignment. The VAPI is pointing at *where* that misalignment is. Your lowest domains are the places where you’re either not executing in a way that serves you, or you’re executing in a direction that doesn’t match your values and your vision.
- So the first step isn’t “do more.” It’s **“name the misalignment.”** You’ve already started that in Lesson 1.1. The next step is to go under the surface—what’s really causing that misalignment? We’ll do that in Module 2.

---

### Part 2 — What it’s cost you not to have this language (pain lever)

**Talking points:**

- For a long time you might not have had words for what was off. So you guessed. You tried tactics. You copied someone else’s system. You told yourself you just needed to be more disciplined, or more confident, or more something. **That’s expensive.** It costs time, energy, and a lot of self-doubt. It can cost relationships, health, and the peace of mind that comes from knowing you’re moving in a direction that actually fits you.
- Now you have a baseline. You have names. You don’t have to guess in the dark anymore. The rest of this course is about going deeper—diagnosing what’s underneath the numbers, feeling the cost of staying where you are, and then seeing what’s possible when you get aligned. No hustle. No “just push through.” Clarity, confidence, and conviction.
