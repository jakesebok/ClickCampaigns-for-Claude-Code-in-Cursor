Pyramid + Circumpunct

The ascending pyramid with the circumpunct — circle + center dot — replacing the apex. The circumpunct is one of the oldest symbols in existence: the sun, the self, the eye of Ra, alchemical gold, the realized soul. At the tip of the pyramid, it transforms the mark from "ascending toward something" into "ascending toward the realized self." Four variations, from most minimal to most expressive.

Variation 1
Floating Above
The circumpunct sits above the pyramid apex, touching it at the base of the circle. The pyramid literally points to it. Clean separation between the two forms — the eye reads pyramid first, then travels upward to the circumpunct. The most legible and composed of the four variations.

What this says

The pyramid reaches upward and arrives at the circumpunct — the realized self. The eye naturally reads bottom-to-top: foundation → structure → self → the sun above it all. Clean, composed, minimal. The dashed tier lines add the architectural/blueprint quality without adding visual clutter. The gold circle and dot are unmistakably different in geometry from the triangular forms, which is what gives the composition its quiet drama.

Variation 2
Apex as Center
The pyramid's apex IS the circumpunct's center dot — the two symbols completely merge at their most important point. The circle radiates outward from the very tip of the pyramid. This creates more tension and integration: the pyramid doesn't point to the circle, it generates it. Alignment creates the sun.

What this says

The pyramid and circumpunct don't coexist — they fuse. The pyramid's single point of precision generates the radiating circle. "When everything converges to a point, it creates light." This version is more dynamic and has more internal tension — the circle pierced by three angular lines feels alive. It also holds together better as a single unified symbol rather than two symbols stacked.

Variation 3
Pyramid Within
The circle becomes the container — the whole, the field, the context of all growth. The nested pyramids ascend within it, with the inner apex touching the center dot. The reading reverses: you begin in wholeness, and within that wholeness you build structure and ascend. Most integrated, most meditative. The circumpunct encloses everything.

What this says

The outer circle is the universe, the whole, the field of life. The pyramids ascend within it — structure within wholeness, not in spite of it. The small circumpunct at the apex is a jewel at the top of the pyramid. This is the most meditative and contemplative reading of the three — it would feel right in a retreat context or on the cover of a coaching workbook. It loses some of the boldness of V1 and V2, gaining depth instead.

Variation 4 — Recommended
The Balanced Mark
V1 with refined proportions: the circumpunct circle stroke weight exactly matches the inner pyramid's stroke weight. The gap between pyramid apex and circle base is precisely equal to the circle's radius, creating perfect visual breathing room. The center dot fills to gold. Everything is in calculated proportion — this is what it looks like when the mark is "finished."

Why this is the finished mark

Four things make V4 the resolved version. One: the circumpunct stroke weight exactly matches the innermost pyramid line, creating visual harmony between the two geometries — they feel like they belong to the same family. Two: the gap between the circle's base and the pyramid's apex is exactly equal to the circle's own radius, which creates the sense of breathing room without separation. Three: the pyramid proportions are sharpened — taller, less squat — which amplifies the upward movement you loved. Four: the dashed tier lines are lighter and further spaced, staying architectural without competing.

The mark now reads as one complete symbol: aspiration, structure, ascension, and the realized self — all in the geometry of sacred architecture.
