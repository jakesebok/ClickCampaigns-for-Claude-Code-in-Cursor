## Part 1: The Core Promise

### 1. The one-sentence promise
The reader will walk away understanding that **the value they hold is truly something they could support themselves with**—that their experiences are valuable and needed in this world, and that by not doing the thing they're passionate about, by not sharing the fire that is unique to them, **they are robbing the world of their truly unique gift**.

### 2. The road ahead
- There is a road ahead that is **known**
- The stumbling blocks are **known**
- They don't have to repeat the mistakes of everyone else who goes into this
- They will feel as though they have **a guide along the way**

### 3. Who this book is for
**Aspiring or successful business owner/entrepreneur/founder** who wants to or has turned their transformation into their vocation.

- Can be in the **building phase** or the **operator phase**—doesn't matter
- They get value either way:
  - **Normalizing:** Looking at Jake's experience and seeing their own reflected
  - **Wayfinding:** A cautionary guide that helps them feel confident moving forward into turning their transformation into their vocation

### 4. What they're reading instead (and what's wrong with it)
A lot of marketing. A lot of "how to scale your business." Not a lot of:
- How to **become the person** who can run a business
- How to take the **first steps with confidence** when you didn't have a model for it
- **Embodying self-leadership**

## Part 2: Your Story & Authority

### 5. The Promethean moment
Jake provided this in detail in the interview-style questionnaires in the Selling Fire documents in Jake's Voice folder. Reference those for the specific scene.

### 6. The refusal to return phase
One of the hardest seasons of his life.

- **Golden handcuffs:** Paycheck, bonuses waved in his face
- **The COO promise:** The letters "COO" made him feel valuable—if he had them, other people would know why he was important too
- **The fall:** Fell flat on his face
- **The realization:** True success had nothing to do with attaining some station that other people found valuable. It was everything to do with **doing what only I can do, embodying my unique ability, living out my special purpose, and giving the world the fire that only I hold.**

### 7. When it finally worked—and the next trial
When it finally worked, he hit the **next trial**: believing it wasn't valuable enough.

- People were telling him he was changing their lives
- He still didn't believe it was enough
- He had to take a step back and realize **what was actually true**

### 8. Client story: "Where do I sign the check?"
Jake met with a man who asked: *"What is the most important thing for you to have in order to step into your next level?"*

- Jake beat around the bush, gave various answers
- What he ultimately came to: **honesty**
- His next level of freedom required **radical honesty**—not only with others, but with himself. Otherwise he would trap himself.
- The man said: *"Jacob asked a lot of people that question and they always tell me something external. But you just said that your next level requires an internal shift. And that is very different and I respect it so much, so I want to be your client. Where do I sign the check?"*

## Part 3: The Framework

### 9. Step-by-step sequence
Yes, there's a clear sequence:

1. **Introduction** — Helps them feel seen and understood; this book is for them
2. **Framing the Hero's Journey** — How it relates to:
   - The way they first received their transformation
   - The journey they're about to undertake (or are in the middle of)
3. **Understanding the value of what they have**
4. **Potential pitfalls along the way**
5. **How trials, tests, and ordeals** help them become the person who can lead their clients and those they serve into bigger places

### 10. This is NOT business coaching
**Personal transformation coaching for business owners, founders, entrepreneurs, and prospective owners/founders/entrepreneurs.**

- Much less about the **how**
- Much more about the **ontological aspects** of becoming the person who can hold and wield the power they have
- This is about **beingness**

### 11. Rejection as Filter + "The Importance of Telling the Truth"
Rejection as a Filter is 100% part of a section Jake has started to outline called **"The Importance of Telling the Truth."**

- It's easy to settle for **acceptance** when what you want is **belonging**
- Acceptance is not the success you want
- By going for what's cheap, easy, and acceptable, you've set a trap—built a cage, stepped into a prison cell and locked the door
- **The only way to true freedom is by telling the truth**
- The only way to know your real value—that who you are is valuable—is by being vulnerable enough to put yourself out there and **watch people flock to you, because your authenticity is magnetic**

## Part 6: Voice & Tone

### 18. How personal to go
**Feel free to get extremely personal.**

- People value the truth
- Don't do it for shock factor
- Do it for **bond-building and normalizing** of real human experiences

### 19. Spiritual language
- The book is **extremely spiritual by nature**
- But Jake does **not** want to speak in spiritual language
- He could reference that the same things have been talked about by traditions and religions through time and space—creates empathy, authority, and levity
- He wants to speak from a **fairly rational standpoint**
- Reference **JAKE-SEBOK-WRITING-STYLE.md** for how he typically positions himself
