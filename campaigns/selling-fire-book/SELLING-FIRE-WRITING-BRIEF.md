## Voice & Tone

### Do
- **Second person ("you")** throughout. Speak directly to the reader.
- **Warm and intimate** — Trusted guide, not lecturer.
- **Invitational** — "What if...?" "Here's my invitation." "Let's..."
- **Grounded** — Acknowledge pain, mess, difficulty. No spiritual bypass.
- **Rational language** — Spiritual by nature, but speak from a fairly rational standpoint. Can reference traditions/religions for empathy and authority, but don't lean on spiritual jargon.
- **Personal** — Extremely personal for bond-building and normalizing real human experience. Don't do it for shock factor.
- **Short + long sentences** — Mix punchy statements with longer, flowing ones.
- **Rhetorical questions** — "You know that feeling?" "What if that's not a flaw?"
- **"Okay?" and "Right?"** — Conversational check-ins where it fits.

### Don't
- Corporate jargon, buzzwords
- Cold or clinical language
- "Fix yourself" or "improve yourself" (prefer "meet yourself," "find yourself")
- Overused self-help clichés without the Jake reframe
- Spiritual language that feels preachy or exclusive

## Metaphors to Use Consistently

- **Fire / Prometheus** — Coming back with fire, hard to hold, hard to transfer
- **Journey / Map / Path** — Road ahead is known, stumbling blocks are known, you have a guide
- **Compass** — Pain, tension, resistance as compass pointing toward what you need
- **Prison / Cage** — What you build when you compromise
- **Mask / Performance** — Who you pretended to be vs. who you really are
- **Treasure / Maze** — At the center: your authentic self, your purpose

## Signature Phrases to Weave In

- "Rejection is a filter."
- "You don't need to fit in everywhere. You just need to belong to yourself."
- "When you create a business that requires you to wake up every day and do something that compromises who you really are, all you've built is a prison."
- "Your life doesn't need to be replaced. It needs to be reclaimed."
- "We're not here to fix ourselves. We're here to meet ourselves."
- "Clarity doesn't come from thinking. It comes from moving."
- "Every step teaches you something new. Every action becomes a vote for the life you're creating."
- "The tension in your life is actually pointing to your joy."

## Teaching Flow (Per Section)

1. **Name the feeling** — "You know that feeling...?"
2. **Reframe** — "What if that's not a flaw? What if it's a compass?"
3. **Authority** — Campbell, Brown, Thurman, Cohen—or myth/pop culture
4. **Story** — Jake's experience or client case study
5. **Application** — "So what does this mean for you?"
6. **Invitation** — "Let's get after it." Forward momentum.

## Chapter Length Guidelines

- **Target:** 6–10 pages per chapter (~1,800–3,000 words)
- **Total:** 24 chapters + intro × ~2,200 words = ~55,000 words ≈ 200–250 pages
- **Draft advantage:** Ch 5 (Effing the Ineffable), Ch 6 (Refusal of the Call), Ch 7 (Money Problem), Ch 9 (Telling the Truth), and Intro have substantial draft material—build from those.

## CTA Placement

- **Chapter 20** — Primary CTA: VAPI assessment, ecosystem, URL
- **Optional** — Light CTA at end of Part II or Part III: "If you want to go deeper, the resources are waiting."
- **Tone** — Invitational, not pushy. "When you're ready."

---

## Structural Reminders

- **Part I** — Reader feels seen, understands they hold something valuable, road is known
- **Part II** — Reader recognizes the traps, normalizes their experience
- **Part III** — Reader feels equipped to move forward with a guide and tools

- **Differentiator** — This is NOT business coaching. It's personal transformation for business owners. Beingness, not how-to.
- **Comp** — The Prosperous Coach (Steve Chandler, Rich Litvin)—similar feel, positioning

## Before You Draft a Chapter

1. Read the chapter outline for that chapter's purpose, key content, and assigned story/case study
2. Skim JAKE-SEBOK-WRITING-STYLE.md for voice
3. Pull any needed material from Selling Fire docs or testimonials
4. Draft in Jake's voice—second person, warm, invitational, grounded
5. Weave in at least one signature phrase or metaphor where it fits
6. End with forward momentum or invitation (except Ch 20, which ends with CTA)
