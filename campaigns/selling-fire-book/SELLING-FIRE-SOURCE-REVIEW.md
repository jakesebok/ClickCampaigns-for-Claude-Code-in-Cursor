---

## Executive Summary

Both documents are rich in Jake's established voice, frameworks, and teaching patterns. The first draft already incorporates many of these—the war cry, Campbell, Thurman, Jonah, rejection as filter, belonging vs. fitting in, the Hero's Journey structure. However, several **signature phrases**, **authority references**, **practical exercises**, and **conceptual distinctions** from these sources are either missing or could be woven in more deliberately to deepen consistency with Jake's published work.

## 3. Conceptual Additions from Journey Home

### 3.1 The 11-Stage Hero's Journey Map (Journey Home)

The Journey Home script uses a **numbered 11-stage structure** that could clarify Ch 1 (Map):

1. The Ordinary World  
2. The Call to Adventure  
3. Refusal of the Call  
4. Meeting the Mentor  
5. Crossing the Threshold  
6. Tests, Allies, and Enemies  
7. The Ordeal  
8. The Reward  
9. The Road Back  
10. The Resurrection  
11. Return with the Elixir  

**Suggestion:** Ch 1 currently describes the journey in broad strokes. Consider adding a brief, explicit list like this—it gives readers a concrete "map" they can reference. The Journey Home also emphasizes: *"Rock bottom = the moment where the pain of staying the same outweighs the fear of change"*—this is already in Ch 6 but could be echoed in Ch 1.

### 3.2 The Three Arcs (Hero Path Blog)

The Hero Path introduces **three arcs** that could structure Part III more explicitly:

- **Arc of Discovery** — Awakening, masks, shadow, discomfort as compass  
- **Arc of Becoming** — Integration, Anima/Animus, Atonement with Father, courage to live from authentic core  
- **Arc of Mastery** — Embodying true self, purpose, leadership, legacy  

**Suggestion:** Ch 14 (Road) previews Part III chapters but doesn't name these arcs. Adding them could give readers a clearer mental model—the draft's Part III maps well onto "Arc of Mastery."

### 3.3 Ahrat vs. Bodhisattva (Hero Path — "Life Is Falling Apart")

The Hero Path distinguishes:

- **Ahrat** — Liberated for their own benefit; hasn't completed the journey to mastery  
- **Bodhisattva** — Turns enlightenment into a boon for all humanity  

**Suggestion:** This distinction could strengthen Ch 21 (Return) or Ch 22 (Selling)—it names the exact trap: transforming for yourself but refusing to bring the fire back. The draft implies this but doesn't name it.

### 3.4 "Two Lists" Exercise (Journey Home)

Journey Home gives a concrete first step:

- **List 1:** Activities, people, environments that leave you feeling *more alive*  
- **List 2:** Activities, people, environments that leave you feeling *depleted*  
- *"That gap reveals the misalignment."*

**Suggestion:** Ch 23 (First Step) or Ch 24 (Invitation) could include this as a specific, actionable exercise—it's simple, memorable, and directly from Jake's teaching.

### 3.5 Five Steps from Journey Home

1. **Notice Your Energy** — The two lists above  
2. **Listen to Resistance & Stop Doing What You Hate** — Dan Sullivan's "procrastination is wisdom"  
3. **Follow Your Jealousy** — "What part of me resonates with that?" (admiration as compass)  
4. **Revisit Childhood Dreams** — "Before you were practical, what did you love?"  
5. **Create a Safe Space to Experiment** — Journal, creative practice, trusted friend  

**Suggestion:** These could be woven into Ch 23 (First Step) or turned into an appendix/exercise section. They're practical, on-brand, and currently absent from the draft.

## 4. Story & Example Additions

### 4.1 "Speaking in Tongues" Story (Hero Path — Masks)

Jake describes faking speaking in tongues at 12 to be accepted—lying, feeling like a fraud, concluding he must be "bad." This is a powerful, specific mask story.

**Suggestion:** Ch 2 (Promethean) or Ch 9 (Truth) could include a brief version. It deepens the "good boy" mask with a concrete, visceral example. The draft has the mask concept but not this specific story.

### 4.2 Restaurant / Son Tantrum Story (Hero Path — Shadow)

The story of Jake's son having a meltdown over butter on pasta, Jake's rage, and the realization that his son triggered the survival mechanism from his own childhood—"if that child yells one more time... we will die."

**Suggestion:** This is in the Hero Path but not in the Selling Fire draft. It's more relevant to the Journey Home/Modern Manhood audience than to Selling Fire's focus on vocation. **Probably skip** unless Jake wants to broaden the book's emotional range—it's powerful but shifts focus from "selling fire" to "parenting/father wound."

### 4.3 Steamy Story / Anima Projection (Hero Path — Anima)

Jake wrote a steamy short story for the Snapchat woman; she said the person he described was nothing like her. He realized he'd been projecting an ideal, not seeing her.

**Suggestion:** Ch 2 (Promethean) currently describes the Snapchat woman as a "mirror" and the realization "I was already whole." The Hero Path version adds the specific anecdote (writing the story, her feedback). Could add one sentence: *"I once wrote her a story—a fantasy—and she told me the person I'd described wasn't her at all. I'd been seeing my projection, not her."* This would align the book with the blog and add a memorable beat.

### 4.4 "Three Lives" Coach Moment (Hero Path — "Life Is Falling Apart")

Jake's coach said: *"It sounds like you've lived two lives already and you're about to begin your third."* Jake describes this as a revelation that blew the top off everything.

**Suggestion:** This could strengthen Ch 19 (Becoming) or Ch 21 (Return)—it names the liminal space between transformation and full embodiment. The draft has the concept but not this specific coach moment.

## 5. Structural & Philosophical Alignments

### 5.1 "Refusal of the Return" vs. "Refusal of the Call"

The Hero Path and Draft 0 interview both mention **Refusal of the Return**—the step where the hero has transformed but won't bring the fire back into the world. The draft focuses heavily on "Refusal of the Call" (Ch 6) but doesn't explicitly name "Refusal of the Return."

**Suggestion:** Ch 6 or Ch 21 could add a sentence distinguishing them: *"I'd refused the call the easy way—so the universe moved me. But there's another refusal: the Refusal of the Return. You've been to the mountaintop. You hold the fire. And you won't bring it back. That's a different kind of prison."* This would align with Campbell and Jake's interview.

### 5.2 "Transformation is 90% awareness" (Journey Home)

*"Transformation is 90% awareness. If I told you you were about to step on a thumbtack, you'd change course. The other 10% is about being honest with what you find and taking action on it."*

**Suggestion:** This could open Ch 23 (First Step) or Ch 20 (True)—it frames the balance between insight and action in a memorable way.

### 5.3 "Baby steps" / "Your authentic self doesn't need to show up everywhere all at once" (Journey Home)

*"I made that mistake and it can get messy. Baby steps."*

**Suggestion:** Ch 23 (First Step) emphasizes bold action. Journey Home tempers that with "baby steps" and "start small." Consider adding a beat: *"Your authentic self doesn't need to show up everywhere all at once. I made that mistake once—it got messy. Baby steps."* This adds Jake's self-deprecating humor and practical caution.

## 9. Summary

The first draft is **well aligned** with Jake's established voice and frameworks. The main opportunities are:

1. **Signature phrases** — A handful of memorable lines from Journey Home and Hero Path could be woven in for consistency.
2. **Authority references** — Erickson, Robbins, Sullivan, Cohen, Michelangelo would deepen the draft's resonance with Jake's other work.
3. **Practical exercises** — The "two lists" and five steps from Journey Home would give readers something concrete to do.
4. **Conceptual distinctions** — Ahrat vs. Bodhisattva, Refusal of the Return, and the three arcs would sharpen the framework.
5. **Minor story beats** — The steamy story (one sentence), the "three lives" coach moment, and possibly the speaking-in-tongues story could add specificity.

**No major structural changes are needed.** The draft's architecture is sound. These are refinements—additions and alignments that would make the book feel more fully integrated with Jake's existing body of work.
