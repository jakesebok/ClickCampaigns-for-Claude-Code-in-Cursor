# Chapter 10: The Charlatans Are Winning

---

I need to warn you about something that's going to make you furious.

At some point—maybe it's already happened—you're going to scroll through social media and see someone doing what you're trying to do. Selling transformation. Coaching people. Changing lives.

Except they haven't changed any lives. They've never been to the mountaintop. They've never stolen fire. They've never died and been reborn. They read a book, took a weekend course, watched a YouTube video about the hero's journey, and now they've got a polished funnel, a six-figure brand, and a waiting list.

And here you sit with actual fire, actual medicine, actual transformation to offer—but you can't figure out how to explain what you do at a networking event.

This is going to piss you off.

Good. Let's talk about it.

---

They've got sales funnels that scale. They've got Facebook ads. They've got "six-figure systems" and "seven-figure blueprints." They speak in bullet points and guarantees. They have before-and-after stories that sound compelling—even if they're inflated, cherry-picked, or outright invented.

They've got everything except the fire.

And people are buying it. Real people with real pain are handing over real money to someone who's never actually been where they promise to take them. People who are genuinely hurting are being sold a painted door that opens to nothing.

You see this and two things happen simultaneously.

First: rage. How dare they. How dare they sell transformation they've never lived. How dare they commodify something sacred. How dare they profit from a process they've never endured while you—the one who actually walked through the fire—can barely articulate what you do.

Second: doubt. Maybe they know something you don't. Maybe the fire doesn't matter. Maybe what matters is the funnel, the ads, the positioning. Maybe the market doesn't actually care about depth. Maybe surface-level wins because surface-level is all anyone really wants.

Both of these reactions are traps.

---

The rage is understandable but useless on its own. Getting angry at charlatans is like getting angry at weeds. They grow where the garden isn't tended. They fill the space that you—the one with actual fire—have left empty because you were too afraid, too humble, or too stuck to step forward.

The doubt is more dangerous. Because if you start to believe that the market rewards inauthenticity—that the only way to win is to become like them—you've already lost. Not the market. Your soul.

Here's what's actually happening.

The charlatans aren't winning because they're better than you. They're winning because they're *visible*. They showed up. They put themselves out there. They didn't wait until they felt ready, didn't wait until they had the perfect offer, didn't wait until the imposter syndrome went away. They just... started. With inadequate fire and excellent packaging.

You, meanwhile, are sitting in a room with the most powerful, life-changing truth you've ever held—and you won't open the door because you're afraid the packaging isn't good enough. Because you're afraid the words will come out wrong. Because you're afraid someone will see through you.

The irony is devastating. The person with nothing to offer is fearless. The person with everything to offer is paralyzed.

---

I'm not telling you to become like them.

I'm telling you to stop letting them win by default.

The world is full of people in pain. They're searching. They're Googling. They're scrolling. They're asking friends for recommendations. And when they search, they find whoever showed up first. Whoever was visible. Whoever had the audacity to raise their hand and say, "I can help."

If that person is a charlatan, it's not because the system is broken. It's because the people with real fire are hiding.

You are the antidote. Not a different flavor of the same product—the *antidote*. You carry medicine that actually works. You've walked the road they only read about. You have scars they can only imagine.

But none of that matters if nobody knows you exist.

---

Here's the reframe I want you to sit with.

Your resistance to the traditional business world isn't weakness. Your inability to care about "workplace productivity" isn't a failure. Your frustration watching charlatans win isn't jealousy.

These are part of your gift.

The reason you can't stomach selling something inauthentic is because you know the difference between fire and a flashlight. The reason you struggle to package your work into neat little bullet points is because what you offer doesn't fit in a bullet point. The reason the charlatans' success enrages you is because you carry the real thing and you know—in your bones—that the people buying their programs deserve better.

That's not a flaw. That's your compass pointing north.

The question isn't how to become more like the charlatans. The question is how to get loud without selling out.

---

And the answer is simpler than you think.

You don't need a better funnel. You need to tell the truth. Your truth. In your voice. Without apology.

The charlatans win on packaging. You win on substance. But substance without visibility is just a journal entry. You need to be visible. You need to show up. You need to open your mouth and say the real thing, even when it's messy, even when it doesn't fit the template, even when the algorithm doesn't reward it.

Because here's what the charlatans can never replicate: conviction born from lived experience.

They can copy your language. They can mimic your framework. They can even approximate your energy for a little while. But they can't fake the look in your eyes when you tell someone their pain has a purpose. They can't replicate the gravity of someone who's actually been to the underworld and back. They can't manufacture the magnetic pull that happens when a human being speaks from a place of total alignment between their words and their truth.

That's your competitive advantage. And it's unbeatable—once you let people see it.

---

One more thing about charlatans, and this one might sting.

Some of them aren't charlatans. Some of them are people just like you—fire-wielders who got tired of hiding and decided to show up imperfectly. People who figured out the packaging before they figured out the depth. People who started before they were ready and learned as they went.

Not everyone who's visible is a fraud. And not everyone who's invisible is holding fire.

Your job isn't to judge them. Your job is to outshine them. Not through louder marketing or flashier branding—but by being so relentlessly, unapologetically *you* that the people who need your specific fire can find you in the noise.

The world doesn't need you to be angry at the charlatans.

The world needs you to stop letting them be the only option.

Show up. Speak. Let the fire do what it was always meant to do.

Burn.
