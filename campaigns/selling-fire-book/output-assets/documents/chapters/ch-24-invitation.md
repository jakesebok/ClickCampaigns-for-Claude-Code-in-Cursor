# Chapter 24: The Invitation

---

We're at the end.

Or rather, we're at the beginning.

---

Let me remind you of the four promises I made at the start of this book—because I want you to hold them up to the light and see if they've been kept.

**Your value can support you.** There's a way to turn your transformation into income. Not by dimming your fire. Not by building a prettier prison. Not by selling something you don't believe in. By telling the truth about what you carry, finding the people who need it, and designing a container that lets the fire do its work.

**By not sharing your fire, you're robbing the world of your unique gift.** The people who need what you carry are out there right now, searching. Struggling. Wearing masks of their own. And they will continue to search—and continue to find charlatans instead of healers—until you step forward.

**The road is known.** The stumbling blocks are mapped. The pitfalls have names: the refusal of the call, the money problem, the prettier prison, the charlatans, the voice of reason, the thermostat, the fishing net. You know them now. They can't ambush you anymore.

**You have a guide.** This book. My story. The stories of others who've walked this path. And—if you choose to go further—an entire ecosystem designed to help you move from clarity into action.

---

I want to be straight with you about what this book can and cannot do.

This book can change the way you see yourself, your fire, and the path ahead. I believe it already has, if you've read it with an open heart.

This book can give you the map. The frameworks. The language. The courage. The permission you never needed but always wanted.

What this book *cannot* do is take the steps for you.

The first step is yours. And only yours.

---

Here's what I know about you—and I know this because you're still reading.

You didn't pick up this book because you were curious about entrepreneurship. You picked it up because something inside you *recognized* it. The title, the premise, the promise—something in you said *yes* before your mind could argue.

That yes is the fire.

It's still burning. It was burning before you opened this book and it'll be burning after you close it. The question was never whether you have fire. The question was whether you'd give yourself permission to let it be seen.

You've spent these pages watching me tell my story—my masks, my prisons, my refusals, my failures, my slow and painful return to truth. I didn't share all of that to impress you. I shared it because I wanted you to know: the person writing this book is not some guru who figured it all out and descended from the mountain with a neat little system.

I'm a person who's walking the same path you are. A few steps ahead in some places, a few steps behind in others. Still learning. Still becoming. Still holding fire that burns my hands some days.

And still showing up.

---

If you want to go further—and I hope you do—here's what I'd suggest.

**Start with the Two Lists.** This is the simplest, most honest exercise I know. Grab a piece of paper. Make two columns.

In the first column, list every activity, person, and environment that leaves you feeling *more alive*. The things that light you up. The moments where time disappears. The people who make you feel like *you*.

In the second column, list every activity, person, and environment that leaves you feeling *depleted*. The meetings that drain you. The relationships that cost more than they give. The tasks that make you stare at the ceiling and wonder how you got here.

That gap between the two lists? That's the misalignment. That's where your fire is being suffocated. And that gap is also your map forward—because what needs to change becomes obvious once you see it written in your own handwriting.

**Join the community.** You don't have to walk this path alone. You weren't meant to figure life out alone. Coaching, mentorship, and community aren't crutches—they're accelerators. There are other fire-wielders out there—people who understand the journey, who speak the language, who know what it's like to hold something sacred and try to build a life around it. Find them. Be with them. Let them see your fire.

**Do the exercises.** Throughout this book, I've offered reflections and practices. If you skimmed past them—that's okay. Go back. Do them. Not because they're homework, but because the fire lives in the *doing*, not in the reading.

**Take the first step.** You know what it is. You've known since before you opened this book. The thing you've been avoiding. The conversation, the post, the offer, the truth.

Do it this week.

---

I started this book with Howard Thurman's words, and I want to close with them too.

*"Don't ask what the world needs. Ask what makes you come alive, and go do it. Because what the world needs is people who have come alive."*

You've come alive. I can feel it through these pages. The fire you carry is real, and it is needed, and the world is darker without it.

So stop hiding it. Stop shrinking it. Stop waiting for someone to tell you it's okay.

Imagine waking up on the page of your life—the page you're on right now—and realizing you're not just a character in someone else's story. You're the author. You've always been the author. You just forgot.

Your authentic life blueprint is your return ticket to yourself—to the life that fits, to the self that's always been waiting for permission to lead.

You know what truth feels like in your body. And you know when you're betraying it. You have a compass. You've always had it.

Prometheus didn't steal fire so he could admire it alone in his cave.

Neither did you.

---

The life you're dreaming of won't just appear. It gets built one bold step at a time. You've already proven you're someone who's willing to take those steps—because you walked through the fire to get here.

The labyrinth is thoroughly known. The thread is in your hand. The heroes of all time have gone before you.

Now it's your turn.

Godspeed, my friend.

Let's get after it.
