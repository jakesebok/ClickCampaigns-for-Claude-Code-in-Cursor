# Introduction: Selling Fire

---

> *Don't ask what the world needs. Ask what makes you come alive, and go do it. Because what the world needs is people who have come alive.*
> — Howard Thurman

---

So, you want to build a coaching business, huh?

You think you've got what it takes to not simply charge other people for your goods or services—but to build something that makes an *impact*. You're going to alchemize your most painful struggles into a panacea for others so they don't have to continue struggling the way you did.

Yeah, I was there once. It's a beautiful moment. Both thrilling and terrifying at the same time. It's the beginning of a massive life change. It *should* be thrilling.

But here's the thing.

Most people who start businesses see an opportunity to make some cash by capitalizing on a rising demand. They're business savvy. They've done the research and run the numbers. People want coffee. They sell coffee. People want fit, toned, healthy bodies. They sell gym memberships.

But you? You're selling a transformation that they don't even know they need yet.

You didn't even mean to stumble upon it. But you did. And you knew without question that this was your calling. You didn't mean to start a business.

And now you have to figure some things out.

---

There's a moment in every transformative experience when you realize you can never go back.

It's not that you choose not to go back. It's that you physically *can't*.

Whether your awakening came from heartbreak, burnout, sobriety, loss, a diagnosis, or the sudden awareness that you've been lying to yourself for years—a part of you woke up that refuses to go back to sleep.

Once you know the truth, you can't unknow it.

You've seen too much, felt too much, become too much to ever squeeze yourself back into the shape you once pretended to be.

This is the Promethean moment.

In the Hero's Journey, it's the point where you've stolen fire from the gods. You hold power beyond your wildest dreams. Your entire life has changed. And you know you can't keep this living flame all to yourself.

You must bring it to the dead and dying masses still languishing in cold darkness. To fail to do so would betray your very purpose. Not completing this mission would be to waste the priceless gift you've been granted. Not rising to the challenge would smack of a wasted life.

There's only one problem.

You've never held fire before and you have no idea what to do next.

---

That's the thing about divinely inspired wisdom. It's really hard to hold and even harder to pass on.

Like Prometheus—whether they chose it or not, whether they're aware of it or not—every conscious soul is the hero of their own epic adventure with their own set of tests, trials, and hidden wisdom to unveil.

It is only the select few who choose to embark on their journey of transformation with intention.

Fewer still make it through the trials that await them without giving up and returning home to safety with nothing to show for their wear.

An even smaller number bring the spoils of their battles back to live fuller, richer lives.

And finally, it is the truly elite minority who get to the end of their journey having paid enough attention to map their path with the level of detail required to mentor and lead others as they traverse the same ground.

This book is written for that elite minority.

---

*Selling Fire* is a book that honors the journey of the Mentor—the soul who dared to undertake a journey of transformation and who knows beyond any doubt that the fire they carry can and must change the world. This book is for the individual who knows their life, their career, their mission is to bring this special knowledge to those who need it most. And who will not stop, at any cost, to complete their sacred work.

The next steps you take will transport you beyond your known world. They will take you into that uncomfortable realm necessary for metamorphosis to occur. You won't simply learn new skills here; you will become something new. And if you're reading this book, you already know that being reborn requires something many are unwilling to endure: death.

The road ahead holds a whole new cycle of thresholds, tests, allies, enemies. New ordeals. New death. Phoenixes rising from new ashes. Secret wisdom. And returning, once again, to the place where you now stand—bearing the boon you need to change the world.

Don't lose heart. Members of this elite have something that others do not: experience.

They have already walked a road thick with dangers. They have already died as the person they thought they were and been reborn as something greater. They have already floated in the gracious rhythms of transformative currents and understand that it is only through surrender that we find our strength. They have already come to terms with the truth that the hardest moments of their lives are the very forge that purified the gold they now possess.

For them, the unknown road ahead is an old friend. And the steps they take, they trod with steadfast intention and purpose.

And though this path can at times be lonely, remember that we are not alone. As Joseph Campbell once said:

> *"We have not even to risk the adventure alone, for the heroes of all time have gone before us. The labyrinth is thoroughly known… we have only to follow the thread of the hero path."*

This book is a map that follows the thread of that Hero Path.

---

In the pages that follow, I'll share some of my story and the stories of others—stories ubiquitous in the lives of founders, coaches, creators, and healers as they embark on the next phase of their journey.

I'll give you insight into the underlying dynamics at play:

- Why your day job feels *more* suffocating after you've grown, not before
- Why your first instinct is to build a "safer," more acceptable version of your calling—a prettier prison
- Why money shame, spiritual bypassing, and old family stories around success can sabotage even the most beautiful business
- Why charlatans so often outrun the deeply gifted—and what to do about it

And I'll give you tools for how to alchemize your demons into your guardian angels.

But first—let me be honest about something.

This book serves not only as a guide for what to expect along your journey but also as my own initiation. My own great Ordeal. The very act of writing the pages you now hold is a step in my own path to Becoming the Mentor.

Because the truth is... the first time you step into mentorship, you've never done it before.

---

Imposter syndrome is one of the greatest obstacles that keeps would-be heroes from sharing their fire in the first place.

Another is that doing so requires whole new sets of skills—coaching skills, teaching skills, business skills. Simply because you possess information does not mean you are equipped to adequately communicate and share it. Being able to share that information does not mean you have the skills to share it in a manner that creates impact.

And even for those who have mastered all of the above—each are worthless if you cannot share them in a manner that keeps a roof over your head and food on your table.

And yes, there's more.

Even for those who've mastered all of that, they might still find themselves wrestling with invisible enemies—shadowy, subconscious saboteurs. For them, more effort will not equal more reward. It will simply result in greater and greater frustration that their "perfectly designed" business isn't executing.

Meanwhile, they watch charlatans make millions selling surface-level bullshit. People who've never been to the mountaintop, never stolen fire, never died and been reborn—but they have "sales funnels that scale." They have Facebook ads. They have "six-figure systems" and "seven-figure blueprints."

And here you sit with actual fire, actual medicine, actual transformation to offer—but you can't figure out how to explain what you do at a networking event.

You're stuck.

Stuck between the spiritual truth of your transformation and the material reality of bills to pay.

Stuck trying to explain the ineffable in LinkedIn-appropriate language.

Stuck watching the loudest voices win while the deepest truths stay silent.

---

This book is for you.

You're the coach who quit their corporate job but can't figure out how to explain what they actually do.

You're the healer who knows they carry medicine but feels fraudulent charging for it.

You're the creator whose true work scares them so much they keep building prettier prisons instead of taking the leap.

You're the awakened soul who gained everything spiritually but lost their ability to play the game that pays the bills.

I'm not here to teach you sales funnels or Facebook ads. I'm here to show you how to take the fire you stole from the gods and transform it into something the marketplace can actually hold—something with a name, a promise, a process, and an outcome.

In **Part I**, we'll map your hero's journey and excavate the exact nature of your fire. You'll discover why your transformation isn't just personal—it's your credential. Your scars aren't just wounds; they're where your purpose lives.

In **Part II**, we'll face the shadows—money shame, imposter syndrome, the temptation to build prettier prisons that still aren't your real work. You'll learn why the charlatans are winning and how to get loud without selling out.

In **Part III**, we'll turn your ineffable truth into something you can actually sell. You'll learn to name your fire, find your people, and design what I call your "first elixir"—a simple, concrete offer that solves a real problem without stretching the truth.

In **Part IV**, you'll discover how to sell fire without selling your soul. How to have enrollment conversations that feel like deep coaching. How to charge premium prices for the transformation only you can provide. How to stay lit when life gets hard and the old world calls you back.

---

Most importantly, you'll learn this:

Your resistance to the traditional business world isn't weakness. Your inability to care about "workplace productivity" isn't a failure. Your frustration watching charlatans win isn't jealousy.

These are part of your gift. And when you stop resisting them and lean into your unique genius, everything else falls into place.

You're not meant to be another life coach with a clipboard. You're meant to be a guide, a mentor, a leader.

The world doesn't need another business book. It needs more people who've been to hell and back, willing to light torches for others still stumbling in the dark.

It needs you to stop hiding your fire and start selling it.

Prometheus didn't steal fire so he could admire it alone in his cave. Neither did you.

The path ahead is dark, winding, and at times lonely. But it is *your* path. And it is the only option for souls who have come this far.

The labyrinth is thoroughly known. We have only to follow the thread.

Godspeed, my friends.
