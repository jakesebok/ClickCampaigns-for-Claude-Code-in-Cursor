# Chapter 15: Trials as Training

---

Every trial you've faced on this journey—every failed offer, every client who said no, every moment of doubt that made you want to crawl back to the safety of a paycheck—was training.

Not punishment. Not evidence that you're on the wrong path. Not proof that you're not cut out for this.

Training.

I need you to reframe the way you think about the hard parts. Because what comes next is going to be hard too. And if you keep interpreting difficulty as failure, you'll quit before the good part starts.

---

In the hero's journey, there's a stage Campbell calls *The Road of Trials*. It comes after the hero has crossed the threshold into the unknown but before they face the supreme ordeal. It's a stretch of tests, allies, and enemies—each one designed to prepare the hero for the challenge that lies ahead.

Luke doesn't walk into the Death Star and destroy it on his first day. He trains with Obi-Wan. He practices with the lightsaber. He learns to trust the Force in small ways before he has to trust it with everything.

Frodo doesn't carry the ring to Mount Doom in a straight line. He stumbles through mines and marshes. He loses allies. He gets lost. Every detour, every setback, every moment of despair isn't a deviation from the path—it *is* the path.

Your trials are the same.

That client who ghosted you after the first session? They taught you something about who you're meant to serve and who you're not.

That launch that flopped? It taught you that the offer wasn't aligned—which is infinitely more valuable than a mediocre success that would have kept you building the wrong thing.

That conversation where you fumbled your words and couldn't explain what you do? It showed you the exact gap between your inner knowing and your outer expression—which is the gap this entire journey is designed to close.

Every trial is a data point. And if you're paying attention, each one moves you closer to the person you need to become.

Every challenge you've faced, every mask you've worn, every mistake along the way... none of it was wasted. It was all *plot development*.

The dragon always guards the gold. That thing you most don't want to face? That conversation, that vulnerability, that terrifying leap? The treasure is behind it. Not in spite of the fear, but *because* of it. Joseph Campbell said, *"The cave you fear to enter holds the treasure you seek."* And Leonard Cohen reminded us that *"the cracks are where the light shines through."*

Those cracks in your life—the failures, the breakdowns, the moments you thought would destroy you—they're not flaws. They're openings. Doorways for something more real to emerge.

---

Here's the part that's easy to miss: the trials aren't just preparing you for *your* success. They're preparing you for *your clients'* success.

Every moment of doubt you've experienced is a moment you'll one day hold space for in someone else. Every breakdown you've survived is a breakdown you'll be able to look someone in the eye and say, "I know exactly how this feels. Keep going."

Your credibility as a mentor isn't built in a classroom. It's built in the arena. Every scar you collect is a tool you'll use to serve the people who need you.

Without the trials, you'd have nothing to draw from. You'd be all theory and no testimony. All map and no road.

The dragon, the boss level, the death star—these aren't just obstacles. They're the curriculum. And you're being trained not just to survive them, but to teach others how to survive them too.

---

I watched this play out with one of my clients in real time.

He came to me during what he called "the worst year of his life." His business was failing. His marriage was strained. He felt like everything he'd built was collapsing. He was convinced it was over—that he'd made a terrible mistake, that he should have stayed in corporate, that the fire was a delusion.

We didn't try to fix anything. We didn't strategize. We didn't optimize his funnel or rewrite his offer.

We sat with the trial.

We asked: what is this teaching you? What part of you is being burned away that needed to go? What is being forged in this fire that couldn't exist without it?

It wasn't an easy conversation. It took weeks. But what emerged from that crucible was a clarity he'd never had before—not about his business, but about *himself*. About who he was becoming. About the specific, irreplaceable thing he carried that no amount of market research could have revealed.

The trial didn't stop him. It *became* him. And the business he built on the other side of it was the one that actually worked—because it was built by the version of him that had been forged in the fire, not the version that was still hiding from it.

---

So when the trials come—and they will—I want you to hold two things in mind.

First: you are not failing. You are being shaped. There is no version of this journey that doesn't include hardship. The question isn't whether you'll face trials. It's whether you'll let them train you or let them stop you.

Second: every ordeal you endure increases your capacity to serve. Every wound you heal in yourself is a wound you'll be able to heal in others. Every dark night of the soul adds another layer of depth to the fire you carry.

Campbell called this *the apotheosis*—the moment where the hero transcends their old self entirely. It doesn't happen in spite of the trials. It happens *because* of them.

You're not just building a business on the road of trials. You're becoming the person who deserves to run it.

And that person—the one being forged in this moment, right now, through the exact difficulty you're facing—is the one your clients are waiting for.

Trust the process. Keep walking.

The fire gets brighter on the other side.
