# Kendall's Questions for Selling Fire

> **From Kendall (Book Ghostwriter)** — I've read through your word-vomit draft, the Selling Fire framework doc, and your personal story. The bones are strong. The Promethean metaphor, the prison line, the confluence of genius + market need—that's the kind of stuff that makes books stick. Before we can build this into 150–300 pages that establish you as the authority, I need to go deep on a few things. Here are my questions.

---

## Part 1: The Core Promise

**1. The one-sentence promise.**  
If someone finishes this book and does the work, what is the one transformation they'll have? Not "they'll have a business"—what will they *be* or *know* or *have* that they didn't before? (e.g., "They'll have a premium offer that converts *and* doesn't compromise who they are.")

**2. Who exactly is this for?**  
You've said mission-driven coaches, healers, creators. Can you get more specific? Age? Stage? (Pre-revenue? $5K/month and stuck? Side hustle while employed?) What's the one thing they all have in common that makes them *your* reader?

**3. What are they reading instead?**  
What books or gurus are they currently turning to? And what's wrong with those—what do those approaches miss that Selling Fire addresses?

---

## Part 2: Your Story & Authority

**4. The Promethean moment.**  
You started answering this in the interview—when was the *exact* instant you knew you had "stolen fire"? Not the general awakening at 31, but the specific scene. Where were you? What were you doing? What broke through? I need the scene so we can open with it.

**5. The "refused the return" chapter.**  
You stayed in the day job, got angrier, got fired (Jonah and the whale). Then you got certified, tried to transfer the fire—and it fell flat. Over and over. What did "fell flat" look like? Specific launches, offers, moments where you thought *this* would work and it didn't? That's the meat of the middle—the failed attempts before the breakthrough.

**6. When did it finally work?**  
What changed? Was it a shift in who you served? How you framed the offer? A belief you had to hack? I need the before/after so we can reverse-engineer it for the reader.

---

## Part 3: The Framework

**7. The Selling Fire methodology—how many steps?**  
From your draft I see: name what you want, use resistance as compass, define who you serve, compelling vision, overcome limiting beliefs, create messaging, build offer that converts. Is there an order? A sequence the reader moves through? Or is it more like interconnected pillars?

**8. The "confluence" moment.**  
You described it as "that ephemeral, abstract, ambiguous, amorphous, impossible to touch confluence" of your genius + market need. How do you actually help someone *find* that? What's the process? The exercise? That feels like the heart of the book—and right now it's the most elusive.

**9. What's the difference between Selling Fire and "business coaching"?**  
You said you didn't want the $5K→$100K scaling playbook. So what *do* you do that's different? Is it that you start with identity/purpose before offer? That you won't let them build something that becomes a prison? I need the contrast sharp.

**10. Rejection as Filter + Tension Compass in the book.**  
These are yours. How do they show up in the Selling Fire journey? Are they tools within the framework? Or are they the *way* you help people find their fire before they try to sell it?

---

## Part 4: Structure & Reader Experience

**11. Teaching vs. memoir vs. both?**  
Do you want this to read like a how-to with your story woven in? Or a memoir that delivers the framework as you lived it? (Think: *Building a StoryBrand* vs. *Man's Search for Meaning*—different feels.)

**12. Exercises and worksheets.**  
Will there be prompts, exercises, or "do this now" moments? If so, how many? And should they be in the book, or in a companion workbook/PDF?

**13. Case studies or client stories?**  
Do you have permission to use anonymized (or named) client transformations? Specific before/afters would strengthen the "this works" argument.

---

## Part 5: Business & Market

**14. What does this book sell?**  
Is the book a lead magnet for coaching? For a course? For the Selling Fire program itself? I need to know so we can plant the right CTAs and position the book to support your business.

**15. Comp titles.**  
What 3–5 books would you put next to this on a shelf? (e.g., *Building a StoryBrand*, *The War of Art*, *The Artist's Way*, *Let Your Life Speak*?) This helps with positioning and title.

**16. Title.**  
Are you married to "Selling Fire"? Or are we exploring? (Subtitle ideas: *Turning Your Transformation Into a Business That Doesn't Compromise Who You Are*? *The Promethean's Guide to Monetizing Your Wisdom*? We can play.)

---

## Part 6: Voice & Tone

**17. How personal do you want to go?**  
You've shared a lot—evangelical upbringing, parentification, marriage, Kenya, divorce. How much of that belongs in this book vs. a future memoir? Selling Fire could stay more focused on the "fire holder" archetype and your professional journey, with personal story as seasoning.

**18. Spiritual language.**  
You use "soul," "calling," "alchemize," "Promethean." How much of that stays? Is this a spiritual book that happens to be about business, or a business book that honors the soul? That affects tone and audience.

---

## What I Need From You Next

Once you answer these—even in rough form—I can:

1. **Draft a detailed chapter outline** (15–25 chapters) with a clear transformation arc
2. **Identify gaps** (what we need to develop or research)
3. **Propose a structure** (Part I: The Fire / Part II: The Transfer / Part III: The Offer, or whatever fits)
4. **Create a writing brief** so we (or I) can start drafting with your voice

Take your time. Some of these you'll know immediately; others might need to percolate. We can do this in rounds—answer what you can now, and we'll go deeper on the rest.

— *Kendall*
