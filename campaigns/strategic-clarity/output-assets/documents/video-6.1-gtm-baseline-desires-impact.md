# Video 6.1 — Baseline, Desires, Impact

**Lesson 6: Goals That Matter (Revenue Bridge) | ~10–15 minutes**

## VIDEO SCRIPT

Lesson 6. This is where the rubber meets the road.

Everything we've done so far—MIQs, WHY, values, Future Four, Reality Check—has been about clarity. Clarity about what you want, who you are, and what you're building toward. Beautiful work. Essential work.

But clarity without numbers is just... well, it's a really inspiring journal entry. And journals don't pay the bills.

So now we turn meaning into math.

---

### The Revenue Bridge

Here's what we're building: a bridge from what you *want* to what your business must *produce*. Not a goal you pulled from a mastermind. Not a number your competitor is chasing. Not "I want to make a million dollars because it sounds impressive."

A Required Revenue number that's built on *your* actual life—your baseline needs, the future you're funding, the desires your MIQs revealed, and the impact your Just Cause demands.

When we're done, you'll know exactly what your business needs to generate—and why. Every dollar will be connected to something real. And when you hit a rough month? You won't be wondering "why am I doing this?" You'll know. Because you built the number on purpose.

---

### Step 1: The Non-Negotiables (Baseline Needs)

We start at the bottom. What must be true for you to feel *stable*? Not thriving—stable. Not stressed. Not lying awake at 2am wondering if you can make payroll.

This is your floor:
- **Owner pay** — the minimum personal take-home to feel stable
- **Taxes** — estimated set-aside
- **Business fixed costs** — rent, software, insurance, contractors
- **Debt obligations** — personal and business
- **Buffer / reserves** — emergency fund, runway, cash cushion

Add it up. That's your Baseline Needs Total.

If you did your Pre-Work Packet, most of these numbers are already there. Just paste them in.

Here's what I want you to notice about this number: it's not exciting. It's not aspirational. It's *necessary*. And most people have never actually calculated it. They're running a business without knowing the minimum it needs to produce for their life to function. That stops today.

---

### Step 2: Fund the Future You (Stretch Desires)

Now we go above the floor.

Remember your Future Four? Personal, Relationships, Skills, Service? Each of those futures costs something. Not in a mercenary way—in a "this life requires investment" way.

For each domain, answer:
- What does "better" look like?
- Why it matters (one sentence)
- Annual investment needed
- First step in the next 30 days

Maybe Personal means hiring a trainer ($6,000/year). Maybe Relationships means a monthly date night and a family trip ($8,000/year). Maybe Skills means a coaching certification ($12,000/year). Maybe Impact means sponsoring a program ($5,000/year).

Add it up. That's your Future You Total.

Then: your MIQ-based stretch desires. Remember those experiences you want? Some of them cost money. The Italy trip. The sabbatical. The house renovation. The bucket-list experience. List 1–5 and estimate the annual cost.

Add it up. That's your MIQ Desires Total.

---

### Step 3: Resource the Impact (Your Tithe)

Your business doesn't have to *be* your Just Cause—but it can *fund* it.

Think of your impact target like a tithe: a percentage you protect, not an afterthought. I suggest 10%, but you choose the number.

Write your Just Cause in plain language. Name a specific impact target for the year. Decide how you'll resource it—money, time, platform, partnerships. Estimate the annual contribution.

That's your Just Cause Total.

---

### Why We Did It in This Order

Notice the order here. It's intentional.

We didn't start with "how much do I want to make?" We started with "what do I need to survive?" Then "what does the life I want cost?" Then "what does impact require?"

Most people set revenue goals based on aspiration or comparison. "I want to make $500K because my friend makes $500K." That's a means goal with no foundation.

We're building a revenue target based on *your actual life*—your needs, your desires, and your cause. Every dollar has a job. Every dollar is connected to something that matters to you.

And here's what's powerful about that: when the quarter gets hard, when the launch doesn't go the way you planned, when the doubt creeps in—you know exactly what you're working for. Not a number on a scoreboard. A life.
