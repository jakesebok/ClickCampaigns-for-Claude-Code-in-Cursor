### Step 4: Required Revenue vs. Target Revenue

Open your worksheet to Step 4. Stack your totals:

- Baseline Needs Total
- Future You Total
- MIQ Desires Total
- Just Cause Total

Add them up. That's your **Required Revenue**. This is the number your business *must* produce for your life to function, grow, and make the impact you want.

Now add a **Reality Buffer**—I recommend 10%. Because business isn't linear. Things break. Launches underperform. Clients cancel. The buffer is what keeps you from being one bad month away from panic.

Baseline + Future + Desires + Impact + 10% buffer = **Target Revenue.**

Write it down. Circle it. This is *your* number. Built on *your* life. Connected to *your* MIQs, *your* values, *your* Future You, and *your* cause.

This is not "I want to make more money." This is "My life requires $X, and here's exactly why."

---

### Step 5: From Revenue to Weekly Behaviors

Now we reverse-engineer. This is where the magic happens—and also where the math gets real.

We're not tracking "leads." I want to be very clear about that. We're tracking **qualified conversations**: real, scheduled, right-fit conversations with people who have intent and capacity. Not tire-kickers. Not "let's grab coffee." Conversations that can result in a yes or a no.

Here's the chain:

**Target Revenue** ÷ **Average Sale Value** = **Sales Needed**

Simple. If you need $300K and your average sale is $10K, you need 30 sales this year.

**Sales Needed** ÷ **Close Rate** = **Sales Conversations Needed**

If you close 30% of your sales conversations, you need 100 sales conversations to get 30 sales.

**Sales Conversations** ÷ **QC → Sales Rate** = **Qualified Conversations Needed**

This is the quality screen. Not every qualified conversation turns into a sales conversation. If 70% of your QCs lead to a real sales conversation, you need about 143 qualified conversations.

Then: divide by 12 for monthly. Divide by 52 for weekly.

143 ÷ 52 = about 2.75 qualified conversations per week.

That's it. That's the number. Everything your life requires—stability, growth, experiences, impact—boils down to roughly 3 qualified conversations per week.

Does that feel doable? It should. Because we didn't start with "you need to do 50 cold calls a day." We started with your actual life and reverse-engineered to a weekly behavior that's achievable.

---

### The Lever Question

Now, what if the math doesn't work? What if you need 10 QCs per week and your capacity says 4?

That's when we pull levers. And the worksheet asks you to choose:

- **Raise price / increase average sale value** — fewer sales needed for the same revenue
- **Tighten offer / narrow scope** — faster delivery, more capacity
- **Change model** — group, retainer, async, productized — one-to-many instead of one-to-one
- **Hire support / delegate** — buy back your time
- **Reduce commitments** — stop doing low-value work that eats capacity

Notice what's *not* on the list? "Work harder." "Add more hours." "Just hustle through it."

That's not how this works. We optimize for alignment *and* sustainability. If the math doesn't fit your life, we change the math—not your life.

---

### Paste the Numbers

Most of the conversion and capacity numbers should be in your Pre-Work Packet already. Paste them in. If you don't have exact numbers, estimate and mark with an asterisk. We can refine later. But having a rough number is infinitely better than having no number.

The key fields:
- Average sale value
- Close rate
- Show rate (booked → attended)
- QC → Sales conversation rate

If you're not sure about your close rate, use 25% as a starting benchmark. If you're not sure about your QC quality rate, use 60%. These are conservative. Better to plan conservatively and overdeliver than to plan optimistically and scramble.

### What This Gives You

When you're done with Step 5, you have something most business owners never have: a weekly scoreboard with one number on it. **Qualified conversations per week.**

That's your leading indicator. Not revenue (that's a lagging indicator). Not leads (that's vanity). Qualified conversations. Because if you hit your QC number every week, and your conversion rates hold, the revenue takes care of itself.

Your AI coach will track this number. When you ask for a weekly plan, it'll include your QC target. When you fall behind, it'll diagnose whether the issue is volume, quality, or conversion—and recommend the right lever.
