# Video 1.2 — Your Life Lists: Worksheet Facilitation

**Lesson 1: The Real Reasons | ~5–10 minutes**

---

## VIDEO SCRIPT

Alright, you've got the distinction. End goals versus means goals. You know what we're after. Now let's actually do the work.

Open up your Life Lists. You'll see three columns: **Experiences I Want**, **How I Need to Grow**, and **How I Will Contribute**. Simple. On purpose. We're not overcomplicating this.

---

### How to Fill This Out

Here's how I want you to approach this:

**Write fast.** Don't filter. Don't edit as you go. Don't try to make it sound good. Just let it pour. Speed produces honesty. Editing produces performance. And we don't need your performance right now—we need your truth.

**Be specific and sensory.** "I want to travel" is a means goal wearing an end-goal costume. "I want to wake up in a villa in Tuscany with my wife, no phone, no alarm, drinking espresso while the sun comes up" — *that's* an experience. See the difference? One is a placeholder. The other is a life.

**Be human.** "I want to be present with my kids at dinner without checking my phone." "I want to feel rested—like *actually* rested—for the first time in three years." "I want to walk into a room full of people and not perform." Those are real. Those are end goals.

---

### Column by Column

Let me give you some prompts to get unstuck. You don't have to use all of these. Pick the ones that hit.

**Experiences I Want:**
- What do I want to feel more often?
- What do I want my days to actually look like?
- Who do I want to be with?
- What do I want to do "just because I'm alive"?
- If money and time were completely handled, what would I do next Tuesday?

**How I Need to Grow:**
- What qualities do I need to embody? (Courage, patience, boundaries, trust, leadership, calm?)
- What skills would make my life simpler?
- What patterns must I outgrow? (People-pleasing? Overworking? Avoiding hard conversations?)
- Who do I need to become to hold the life I described in column one?

**How I Will Contribute:**
- Who do I want to serve, and how?
- What impact do I want to be known for?
- What problem makes me angry enough to dedicate years to solving it?
- If I could only help one type of person with one type of problem for the rest of my career, who and what?

---

### What to Watch For

As you write, you might notice something: your brain will try to sneak means goals in. It'll say things like "I want to make $500K" or "I want to build a team of 10" or "I want 50,000 followers."

When that happens, don't delete it. Just ask: "Why? What's underneath that?" Keep asking until you hit the experience, the feeling, the human thing.

"I want to make $500K" → Why? → "So I can take summers off with my kids." → *That's* the experience. Write the summers-off-with-kids part. The $500K goes in From Meaning to Math later.

---

### The Reflection

After you fill out the three columns, there's an optional reflection at the bottom:

*"What's one means goal you've been chasing that doesn't actually satisfy the end goal underneath it?"*

Sit with that one for a minute. You don't have to share it with anyone. But naming it—even just to yourself—is powerful. Because once you see it, you can't unsee it. That's the compass recalibrating. That's you stopping the trade of your life for the wrong thing.

---

### When You're Done

Don't try to organize or prioritize what you wrote. Just let it sit. We're going to use this raw material in the lessons ahead—your Driving Fire is hiding in these answers, your values are woven through them, and From Meaning to Math will be built on top of them.

For now, your only job was to be honest. If you did that, you nailed it.

I'll see you in Lesson 2, where we go find your Driving Fire.

Let's get after it.
