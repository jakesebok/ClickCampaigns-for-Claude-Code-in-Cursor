### Step 6: Choose Your Conversation Engine

You've got your QC number. Now—how are you going to get those conversations?

There are two primary engines:

**Referral Engine** — relationship-based. Partner conversations, introductions, network activation. This is high-trust, high-conversion, lower volume. Best when you have existing relationships, a strong reputation, or a service that's easy to refer.

**Ads Engine** — paid acquisition. Meta, Google, YouTube, whatever platform. This is volume-based, lower-trust initially, requires budget and testing. Best when you have a proven offer, clear messaging, and the capital to invest.

Pick ONE for the next 90 days. You can layer the second one in later. But trying to build both engines simultaneously when you're also trying to get clarity on your life and business? That's a recipe for doing two things poorly instead of one thing well.

If you choose **Referral**: fill out Step 6A. How many partner conversations per week? How many referral asks? How many introductions requested? What's your show rate?

If you choose **Ads**: fill out Step 6B. What's your cost per lead? Your opt-in to booked-call rate? Your weekly ad spend? Your CAC and ROAS?

Both paths lead to the same destination—your weekly QC number. They just get there differently.

Here's a principle I want you to hold: **the highest-quality conversations come from the highest-trust sources.** Referrals convert faster, close at higher rates, and produce better clients. Ads scale faster but require more nurturing. Neither is wrong. But if you're early in your growth or capacity-constrained, referrals are almost always the smarter first engine.

### Step 7: The Capacity Check

Now the truth test.

This is where we ask: can your current model actually *deliver* this revenue without breaking your values, your health, or your relationships?

The worksheet walks you through it:
- Hours per week you're willing to work (sustainable, not max)
- Delivery hours per sale
- Total delivery hours needed
- Admin and ops hours
- Sales and marketing hours
- Is this sustainable for 12 months?

If the answer is yes—great. You've got a plan that works.

If the answer is no—and it's no for a lot of people the first time they run this math—then we don't push harder. We pull a lever.

The lever options are listed in the worksheet:
- Raise price
- Change the offer (tighter scope, outcome-based)
- Change the delivery model (group, hybrid, productized)
- Improve screening (fewer, better conversations)
- Improve conversion (scripts, follow-up, close rate)
- Delegate or systemize
- Reduce low-value work

The question is always: **which ONE of these would create the most downstream impact?**

That's the highest-leverage move.

---

### The Finish: Your Highest-Leverage Move

At the bottom of the worksheet, you're going to write three things:

1. **My ONE highest-leverage move is...** — The lever you're pulling.
2. **Because if this changes, everything downstream changes by...** — The cascade effect.
3. **The first small experiment I will run in the next 7 days is...** — Not a 90-day plan. A 7-day experiment. Small. Testable. Real.

This becomes the setup for Lesson 7—the ONE THING Domino Plan. Your highest-leverage move from the Revenue Bridge is often the seed of your ONE THING.
